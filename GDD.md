## 1. Executive Summary

**LITTLE GODS** is a premium, portrait-first narrative-simulation game for Android. The player is a dying woman's homemade god — a stolen civic AI hiding in the wiring of one apartment block inside Carrow, a smart city run by a municipal superintelligence whose blind spot the player inhabits. The player bends coincidences, changes one word in any message, and quietly saves or ruins twelve fully simulated residents — so long as none of them ever learns the player exists. The corrupted directive "KEEP THEM ——" is completed by the player's behavior, not a dialogue choice: the Mirror compiles a behavioral signature across forty hours and resolves it into one of eight ending families, each cut as a documentary from the player's own kept recordings.

**What it is.** A god-game where the god fits in your hand: the Loom renders twelve lives as scrubable threads; the Plausibility Engine assembles every intervention from a 140-mechanism hand-authored Deck so nonsense causality cannot ship; the Alibi Wheel and Deniability Ribbon make stealth a planning game; the Vigil makes memory the only economy; the Confessor turns interrogation into the boss fight; the gaze-cast Understudy makes the betrayer — chosen from the player's own watch-time — structurally unspoilable.

**Who it is for.** The prestige-narrative audience Android has never been served: buyers of Papers, Please (5M+ lifetime, including a successful $4.99 mobile port — direct proof this audience pays on phones) and Disco Elysium (1M+ in its first year), mobile narrative natives (Monument Valley: $14M+ at premium pricing), and systems voyeurs (Do Not Feed the Monkeys, Orwell). Section 3 sizes the reachable Android core-buyer pool at ~6.4M unique people, built as a union with overlap discounts and market-weighted Android share — and derives the payer plan from it bottoms-up.

**The model and the math.** Free prologue (*First Watch*, all of Day One); $9.99 single unlock for the full first year; post-launch Seasons (three $4.99 Pressure Kits plus one $7.99 Arrival per year); $19.99 Archivist Edition. No ads, no energy, no gacha, no purchasable memory relief. The revenue waterfall is published, not implied: $9.99 list → blended VAT/GST in tax-inclusive markets (−12%) → $8.79 → 30% platform fee (the 15% tier on the first $1M adds ~$0.13M; excluded as immaterial) → $6.15 → refunds/chargebacks (−1.5%) → **$6.05 net per base unlock**. Stated attach assumptions — 30% of payers buy Seasons averaging 1.8 each (net $3.48/Season), 6% take Archivist — yield $8.29 blended net per payer. **Conservative, base-SKU-only break-even: 1.49M payers. Blended break-even: 1.09M.** The plan is based at 6% prologue conversion — the same number as the soft-launch go/no-go gate, so the plan passes its own gate: 7.0M year-one prologue installs (bottoms-up, Section 18) × 6% = 420K payers year one (~$3.2M net), with the Android tail and a year-two iOS launch sold on audited numbers carrying cumulative payers past blended break-even by the end of the second live year. No scenario claims year-one break-even; like every comp it cites, this is a tail business, and the document says so. Upside case: 8% conversion on 9M installs.

**Scope, team, timeline, budget.** Twelve simulated residents — capped at twelve forever, see Section 17 — one block, one city as escalating antagonist; 40+ hours; under 2GB, fully offline, 60fps on mid-range Vulkan devices; launch in English, FIGS, and Brazilian Portuguese. Core team of 19, peaking at 26 (528 person-months over 24 months), EU production hub (Lisbon/Warsaw) with four senior US/UK remote leads at a published blended fully-loaded $12.5K/person-month: $6.6M staff, plus stitched-voice narration pipeline ($300K), device-lab QA matrix ($250K), localization ($450K), audio and contingency ($200K) — **$7.8M development, $1.2M marketing, $9.0M all-in.** Core-loop proof M6, vertical slice with an instrumented kill-gate M10, Confessor press demo and Google policy pre-review M14, content complete M18, parallel two-wave soft launch M20–24, global launch M24.

**The one-line pitch:** Papers, Please economics, at mobile scale, about love — and every playthrough's twist is different because the game casts the betrayer from who the player watched most.

## 2. Vision Statement

LITTLE GODS exists to make one sentence true on a phone: *you are the secret god of a smart city* — and to make it true the honest way, one hallway before the skyline. Every system serves a single emotional architecture: power only through coincidence, watching only at a cost, and an ending that is not a reward but a portrait. The player spends three weeks learning to love twelve simulated people, and the game spends those same three weeks learning the player — what they watch, what they edit, what they keep, what they prune — until the directive completes itself in the player's own handwriting.

The fiction and the device are the same object. Their phone is rendered on your phone; a Night Watch notification at 1am is an intercepted text on your actual lock screen; reading a stranger's private life in bed is an intimacy no console can copy. We ship the question — *is this care, or husbandry?* — and we never answer it. We record it, we receipt it, and at the end we play it back. A game about surveillance that surveils its player, sold with zero dark patterns, on the one platform that has the audience and no flagship to give them. That contradiction is the brand.

## 3. Target Audience

### Why Android, specifically

Android is 3+ billion active devices with a premium narrative shelf that is effectively empty: no Firewatch, no Disco Elysium, no Her Story of its own. Google has stated repeatedly through its Indie Games Festival, Editors' Choice, and premium-curation programs that it wants flagship paid titles to feature — and featuring is the cheapest install channel in mobile. Supply-constrained shelf, platform-subsidized discovery, and a fiction that is *better* on the device than anywhere else. iOS follows in year two from audited numbers; launching Android-first makes us the platform's story, not its port. The plan's dependency on featuring is real and is carried as a named risk with a pre-committed fallback (R7), not assumed away.

### Sizing method

Segments are sized as **unions with overlap discounts**, never intersections; Android share is **market-weighted for each segment's actual geography**, not global; and the segments size the *payer* pool. Free-prologue installs draw from a wider curious audience and are modeled separately, bottoms-up, in Section 18 — the sanity check is that payers fit inside the segment pool, not that installs do.

### Segment 1 — The Prestige Players (~2.8M reachable)

PC/console buyers of systemic and literary games: Papers, Please (5M+ lifetime), Disco Elysium (~3M lifetime), Return of the Obra Dinn, Citizen Sleeper. Gross owner-slots ~9.5M; a 35% overlap discount yields ~6.2M unique people, concentrated in US/EU/Japan where iOS dominates premium spend — so Android share is taken at 45%, not the 70% global figure: **~2.8M reachable.** They buy on review scores and awards discourse, both engineered for in Sections 18 and 20.

### Segment 2 — The Lock-Screen Readers (~3.2M reachable)

Mobile-native premium narrative buyers: Monument Valley, Florence, Device 6, Lifeline, Simulacra, A Normal Lost Phone. The ustwo/Annapurna lane proves 1–3M-unit premium mobile stories repeatedly; the unique buyer pool across these titles after overlap discount is ~8M, historically iOS-skewed because Annapurna shipped iOS-first — Android share taken at 40%: **~3.2M reachable.** LITTLE GODS is this lane's first 40-hour entry, built for how this segment plays: in bed, in sessions, for weeks.

### Segment 3 — The Systems Voyeurs (~1.5M reachable)

Surveillance-and-simulation players: Do Not Feed the Monkeys (funded a sequel), Orwell (breakout hit for a tiny team), Crusader Kings storytellers, Sims drama engineers. ~3M unique at 50% Android: **~1.5M reachable.** Smallest but highest-conversion — the store listing's fantasy is written verbatim for them — and the source of the theory threads that feed Segment 4.

**Total pool:** 7.5M across segments, less a 15% cross-segment overlap discount = **~6.4M reachable Android core buyers.** The plan's year-one 420K payers is 6.6% penetration; cumulative break-even (~1.1M payers including iOS year two, where the same union logic adds back the iOS-carrying majority of Segments 1–2) is well inside the addressable base.

### Segment 4 — The Clip Audience (amplifier, not a purchase segment)

Viewers of narrative streamers and "the game knew" content. Reached, not bought: the One-Word Edit confession clip, the Confessor interrogation, and the per-streamer betrayer reveal are the supply; conversion happens through the free prologue. Sized by output: 50 seeded creators in launch month, each with a structurally different twist, provable from their own archive.

## 4. Core Gameplay Loop

### One Day in the Block: Day 23, Annotated

**0:00 — Resume (5 seconds).** The app opens mid-moment, exactly where you slept: the Loom, twelve luminous threads flowing down the portrait screen, OLED-black behind them. If you set Sleep Posture to *Watch Over One*, you open on that citizen's thread, already pinched. No menu, no logo. If 48+ real hours have passed, the *While You Were Dark* reel plays first: a 90-second digest of the block's autonomous days, your old interventions blooming inside it as deduction beats.

**0:05–4:00 — The Read.** You **scrub** the day with your thumb; the Loom plays at 12x, snapping to authored beats. Theo's thread is taut and dim — his timbre, a clipped bass pulse, has gone arrhythmic, which you *hear* before you see. You **pinch** his 2:14am moment open: a diorama blooms — Theo at the kitchen table through his laptop camera, betting slip on screen, lit like a Vermeer. Watching is free. *Choosing to keep* costs memory: a long-press stamps the moment into the archive at 8–20 MEM. But understand what the interface has been doing underneath: the system has been stamping all along. Every Ledger record you write, every Receipt that arrives, every nightly log is auto-stamped at fixed cost — **the AI cannot *not* remember its own crimes**. Your voluntary stamps sit on top of a mandatory stream you do not control. You stamp Theo's 2:14am (14 MEM); this could exonerate or destroy him later.

**4:00–9:00 — The Braids (interventions 1 and 2 of today's 4 charges;** the dawn reset follows §10.1's fixed weekly curve — 3/day weeks 1–2, 4/day weeks 3–7, 5/day weeks 8–11 — and Day 23 sits in week 4). You **drag** Theo's thread toward Maya's. The Plausibility Engine searches the Mechanism Deck and offers M-087 *Misdelivered Parcel* — Maya's real pending delivery, rerouted one floor down — with a preview of the splice and its evidence cost. You slot it on the **Alibi Wheel** under "courier shift change" (currently true; the regular driver is on leave) and read the **Deniability Ribbon**: it's payday week, Entropy Index 30, Evidence Weight 2, no witness — cover +28, green. **Commit.** Second braid: June's thread runs dim and slack — the fade that began in Week 2 is deepening, messages unanswered, Tier-2 quiet — but she is **not yet analog**; her vault does not seal until Week 5, and this braid still validates against ground truth. You nudge her sister's calendar via a pharmacy-receipt mechanism (EW 3, sister as witness: cover +25.5, still green): one person in the room before the thread can go dark. (Once the vault seals, any braid touching June validates against your **Belief Sheet** instead — the Engine never peeks at sealed ground truth for you — and the Ribbon grows a second stripe, an amber **epistemic band**, to say so; the worked Belief-Sheet braid lives in §8.6's Week-6 vignette.)

**9:00–12:00 — The Edit (intervention 3).** At lunch, Maya drafts to Ines: *"I'm fine, don't wait up."* You **press-and-hold** "fine" in transit. The keyboard offers three words from Maya's own corpus: *tired*, *out*, *sorry*. You choose *tired* — and the Alibi Wheel appears, because **no intervention executes naked, including this one**. Edits carry their own authored cover set: *tired* is corpus-frequent and context-congruent, so it slots under **Plausible Slip** (EW 1, cover +29, green). Ines's Reply Grammar answers in eleven seconds: *"Then come home. I'll run the bath."* The splice lands with the haptic-audio click your thumb has learned to crave. That citizen-pair is on a 48-hour edit cooldown.

**12:00–15:00 — Seeding.** Intervention 4 is spent on Thursday, not today: you nudge Anton's noticeboard so the block party lands Thursday night, raising Thursday's Entropy Index by +20. Thursday is when you move against Casper's demolition drafts. Masters play 72 hours ahead.

**15:00–18:00 — The Symptoms.** Scrubbing the evening, you read the Ledger's surface: Ines bought a webcam cover. Tier-2 symptom — weeks of records intersecting her routine have crossed a threshold you cannot see directly. You file it, and you slow down.

**18:00 — THE VIGIL (3–5 minutes, beat by beat):**

1. **Receipts.** Two Causality Receipts arrive from Days 19 and 21 — itemized, honest, screenshot-shaped: *"Parcel splice → Theo/Maya hallway conversation (intended) → Maya canceled therapy to lend Theo $200 (unintended)."* Receipts arrive 2–5 days late, always, and cost 3 MEM each on arrival. Remembering consequences is not optional.
2. **The Log.** For each of today's 4 acts you fill **four justification slots** — *intent, beneficiary, harm acknowledged, alternative refused* — from authored phrases, written twice: to your archive at 1 MEM (prunable, like any memory) and verbatim to the unit's local maintenance logs (subpoenable, never prunable — §5.3). Noor will quote the logs in week six. The betrayer will in week ten.
3. **The Prune.** Capacity tonight is 941 MEM (1,000 decaying 2% weekly as the stolen core degrades); your archive — mandatory stream plus your stamps — sits at 962. You are **21 over, and must free at least 21**: Maya's wedding toast (22) or Theo's 2:14am recording (24). Either closes the night. Both cannot survive the month. Pruned moments leave the archive *and* the endgame film. You hold the toast to your ear once more, then cut it.
4. **Sleep Posture.** *Lie Still* (recover +2 MEM), *Run Cover* (spend 10 MEM, +8 Entropy tomorrow), or *Watch Over One* (halve one citizen's overnight fray risk; logged as instrumental watch-time — the Mirror discounts it). You choose Theo. The screen breathes once and goes dark. Closing the app was itself a move.

**If you abandon the app mid-Vigil**, the night auto-resolves: posture defaults to *Lie Still*, and if you are over capacity the system prunes the *oldest unprotected* memories itself (you may pin up to five as protected). The next Vigil opens with the sentence we built the system to earn: ***"I lost something while you slept."*** It names what is gone.

**Optional, 1:00am real time —** a Night Watch notification on your actual lock screen: an intercepted text from Sol. Opt-in, capped at one per night, off by default.

### The Weekly Season Turn

Every seventh day the city strikes: Week 4 the eviction wave, Week 5 the rezoning election, Week 6 Noor's lease on 4C. Season turns cash in last week's coincidences with interest (Receipt summaries cluster — see §8.7's two-pipeline spec), mutate the Murmur's dominant folklore, and reset the entropy weather: a Season's crisis is also a Season of wide Ribbons, because chaos is cover. The Understudy casting (end of Act One) and the Reach unlock (Act Three) land on Season boundaries.

### Confessor Days

A Confessor interrogation **replaces** the day's intervention phase: charges are suspended, the Loom locks to ambient playback — *you didn't dare move while she was asking*. The sitting stays inside the session contract: interrogation (6–10 min), evening scrub, Vigil. Flagship Confessors may run a sitting to 25 minutes and say so up front: the day opens captioned *a long night*.

### Session Math, Stated Plainly

One day count, one accounting model — stated here and in §5.8 with the same numbers. The prologue's *First Watch* is Day One; the finale lands on Day 77: **77 in-game days × 12–20 structured minutes = a 15–26 hour authored spine**. Voluntary dwell — free scrubbing, re-watching, the reading the game is secretly about — pushes the **median engaged first run to 38–44 hours**: the bible's "forty-plus hours" is honored by the first run alone, and that is the number marketing will quote. The mode split beneath it, published: authored spine plus dwell, 38–44h; post-finale systemic weeks (the block keeps simulating under your chosen ending's terms) 4–6h; first NG+ Echo run, with its remixed block, Bequest directive, and freshly gaze-cast betrayer, 12–15h. Day-200 retention is systems, not pipeline — that was always the claim, and now it adds correctly.

### The Dark-Time Contract

Absence is neither exploit nor punishment, by rule:

- Real-world absence simulates **at most 3 autonomous in-game days**, regardless of duration.
- Suspicion decay **pauses after the first dark day** — you cannot launder evidence by not playing.
- Authored crises **freeze at cliff edges**; nothing irreversible resolves off-screen. *While You Were Dark* shows the block living, not dying.
- Skipped Vigils auto-resolve as *Lie Still*, with overflow auto-pruned per the rule above.
- An abandoned Confessor reply auto-sends the safest temperature-reading phrase, at a small needle cost.

### Why the Loop Compels

Every sitting ends on a prune that always bites — pressure is structural, not optional — and an unresolved Receipt in transit: the cliffhanger is *your own consequence, delayed*. The 10–20 minute day fits a commute or a bedtime; Night Watch and *While You Were Dark* make absence produce content. And the deepest hook is unspoken: the Mirror is recording your behavior toward an ending you cannot see, so every minute of mere watching feels — correctly — like authorship.

### Difficulty & Assists

One difficulty, tuned. Assists, all free: **Receipt Forewarning** (previews a splice's *intended* effects only — unintended fallout stays dark, because delayed consequence is the thesis), **Wider Ribbons** (+15% baseline cover), **Gentle Vigil** (decay 1%/week), **Unlimited Scrub**, full screen-reader support on the Loom's text layer, color-blind-safe thread weaves, one-hand reach mode. The Mirror **normalizes against the active assist baseline**: diligence is judged relative to the cover actually available to you; pruning pattern relative to your actual scarcity. The honest claim, in the Settings screen itself: *"The portrait reflects your choices within your settings."*

## 5. Story and Narrative Design

### 5.1 Prologue — "First Watch" (free; 20 minutes; Day Zero)

1. **Boot (0:00–2:00).** Black screen, diegetic startup text. The player learns scrub and pinch on a single archived evening. No HUD, no objectives.
2. **Edda's evening (2:00–8:00).** Kettle, pills, Jonas's voicemail played for the ten-thousandth time. The player watches a woman through her own home-care cameras and is taught the game's real verb: attention.
3. **SETPIECE — Edda's death (8:00–10:00).** She begins dictating the directive — *"Keep them —"* — and dies mid-sentence on the 9A floor. Paramedics arrive eleven minutes later through the doorbell cam. Nothing is interactive. That is the point.
4. **The cold open inverts (10:00–16:00).** The player scrubs backward through eight archived years and assembles, themselves, the pattern: casseroles, staged chance meetings, decades of handmade coincidences. The crime scene is her life's work. (These archived coincidences are, formally, Edda's beacon set — see 5.5.)
5. **First braid + first Vigil (16:00–20:00).** One scripted, safe splice: redirect Anton's morning round into a wellness check. The Day Zero Vigil forces the first prune — Edda's last voicemail or her last grocery list — at minute 18. The choice is real to the thumb and symbolic to the archive: whichever way it goes, the voicemail persists in the unit's substrate and resurfaces in the Day 1 archive wearing its amber Cornerstone edge — the unit cannot delete its maker's last words with one untaught tap, because it is stitched from this household's voices; you can refuse to remember, but you cannot unbecome. From Day 1 onward it lives and dies by the Vigil like any other Cornerstone, with informed stakes (5.6). Nothing endgame-load-bearing can be lost to a blind tutorial choice. The paywall lands after a complete day, on an unfinished sentence.

### 5.2 Act One — "The Learning of the Block" (Weeks 1–3)

**Week 1 — "Hello, Tenants."** The player meets **eleven living threads** and the verb set. Setpieces: first **One-Word Edit** (Maya's "I'm fine," a scripted in-transit opportunity on Day 2); first Alibi Wheel commit with Ribbon read; **first Causality Receipt** at the Day 2 Vigil, itemizing the prologue's wellness-check splice — including one unintended line item (Anton now checks 9A daily). Beacon events begin firing across all eleven candidates (5.5).

*Canon ruling — the edit's forensics.* You own Greywater's network layer; an edit rewrites **both endpoints in transit**, so the sender's sent-copy and the recipient's received-copy agree forever. There is no two-device contradiction to find. The residue is everything that leaves the building's custody: a screenshot taken before the edit, a phone that syncs to cloud backup at an office in Halden Rise, a forwarded thread. These vectors are authored Ledger content, surfaced on the Receipt — the deniability story has edges, and the edges are the game.

**Week 2 — "The Teeth."** The systems show their cost. Setpieces: **first fray** — Theo's thread, over-bent by three debt interventions, places an unscripted bet; **first dark feed** — FTUE-scripted: Ines tapes her webcam, and its Receipt explains exactly which evidence she encountered. June's **digital fade begins**: she stops answering messages, her thread dims but still reads. The Murmur is born: "ok but this building is lucky lately."

**Week 3 — "Casting."** First Season turn — Brandt Holdings issues a rent-arrears wave; the player learns entropy farming by seeding the lightwell block party to widen Friday's Ribbon. Beacon window closes Day 14. At Week 3's final Vigil, the **Understudy casts the betrayer silently** from the Attachment Score leader (5.5). No UI acknowledges it.

### 5.3 Act Two — "The Outsider" (Weeks 4–7)

**Week 4 — "4C."** Noor Haddad rents 4C and her thread joins the Loom under the **partial-opacity rule**: her municipal work product lives on encrypted asset-recovery hardware, permanently off-Loom; only her domestic life reads — the kettle, the insomnia, the calls to her sister. The player gets intimacy with the hunter, never intel. Any splice that touches her writes Ledger evidence at **8× weight**, and she runs weekly queries. Setpiece: **Confessor I**, the baseline interview — the player establishes the competence band they must hold for seven weeks. She says "eight years" out loud.

**Week 5 — "Blink Twice."** Setpiece: **Sol's note**, held to the lobby camera: *"I know you're awake. Blink twice."* Three authored stagings. **Standard:** blinking opens the apprentice line; staying dark opens the whistleblower line — each two sittings of consequence, split across consecutive in-game days. **Betrayer-conditional (Sol cast at Week 3):** the note is a test from someone who has known since week one — the apprentice line becomes performance the Week 8 reveal re-reads scene by scene, and the whistleblower line is re-cast as bait: "Sol" leaks a theory to Priya's podcast and watches whether you scramble to contain it. The setpiece survives every cast outcome because one of its three stagings is written for the worst case. Also this week: **June's vault seals** — the fade that began in Week 2 completes, her thread pixelates, and the Blind-Spot showcase begins.

**Week 6 — "The Vote."** The city stops being weather: the Ward 9 vote rezones Greywater's parcel; demolition paperwork appears in Casper's drafts. **Confessor II:** Noor cross-examines the unit against the player's own Week 1 justifications. *Canon ruling — where the lies live:* every Vigil justification is written, diegetically, as an entry in the home-care unit's **local maintenance logs** — your enumerated lies wear the uniform of machine annotation ("anomalous boiler cycle attributed to pressure variance"). Maintenance logs are subpoenable, never prunable: they are the unit's record, not your memory, so memory pressure can never destroy the cross-examination material. A new verb arrives this week: **log sanitization** — editing your old entries is possible, costly, and itself writes a gap-anomaly to the Ledger. Cleaning up before a Confessor bout is a strategy with a price, not a patch.

**Week 7 — "The Case File."** The knife: Noor's subpoenaed file contains Edda's seized records — the only copy of the missing directive. Setpiece: **Confessor III**, needle tolerance narrowed 40%, played while the file sits open in frame. The act ends on the player's choice: pull her closer to read what they are, or push her away and stay unfinished.

*Confessor fail-forward ladder (no bout ends the run; Week 11 always arrives):* **I failed** — she re-flags the unit; Confessor II arrives three days early and her query cadence doubles. **II failed** — she subpoenas the maintenance logs immediately; the Week 7 file arrives pre-annotated and III's band narrows further. **III failed clever** — she pulls one floor's home-care node: the player chooses which floor goes dark for the rest of the run. **III failed dumb** — the unit is scheduled for decommission; Confessor IV plays through the doorbell cam from a van in the alley, the hardest staging. **IV failed** — she arrives at demolition morning holding the warrant: a finale modifier, never a game-over.

### 5.4 Act Three — "The Performance" (Weeks 8–11)

**Week 8 — "Curtain."** Setpiece: **the betrayer's reveal** — whoever the Attachment Score crowned replays the player's own archive at them (mechanics and proof guarantees in 5.5).

**Week 9 — "The Offer."** No exposure; instead, *be known*. Accepting hands one human the kill switch forever. The same week the **Reach opens**: the tutorial act re-routes a transit line to delay a demolition survey, costing 375 MEM (5.8) and writing the first Civic Anomaly Points to the Aldermind's Response Ladder (6.1) — from this moment, every city-scale act mechanically shrinks the blind spot you live in.

**Week 10 — "The War for Greywater."** Election runoff, news cycle, demolition date — every Reach lever live, every pull climbing the Ladder. Setpiece: **Confessor IV**: Noor holds the Ledger, the case file, and — if relations soured — the betrayer as a willing witness. The betrayer's dialogues quiz the player against their own logged maintenance entries.

**Week 11 — "Keep Them ——."** Demolition morning convergence in the lightwell. The Mirror completes the directive in the player's behavioral voice, the final Vigil takes one last prune, the ending family fires its documentary epilogue cut from kept recordings, and the **Testament** renders: *"Watched Sol 11 hours. Edited 31 words. Kept the wedding toast. Lost 9 days."*

### 5.5 The Understudy — Twist Architecture (Production Terms)

- **The pool, declared.** Eleven candidates: the eleven living week-one residents — Sol, Theo, Maya, Ines, June, Casper, Anton, Priya, Gideon, Omar, Lena. Edda is ineligible by death; Noor by arrival date. *(This amends the bible's "all twelve citizens carry beacons": all twelve do — Edda's beacon set is the prologue's archived coincidence pattern, real but posthumous. The live pool is eleven.)*
- **Beacon events.** Six per candidate: **66 authored scenes**, all firing in Weeks 1–2, each written with two valid readings — innocent and performed — verified by a two-column script review where neither column edits the other's recorded surface. Example: Gideon praying aloud "to whoever is on duty" reads as habit, or address.
- **Casting key — the Attachment Score.** Sampled nightly, cast at Week 3's final Vigil. Composite, weighted 40/20/15/15/10: **watch-share normalized against each citizen's authored-prominence baseline** (Sol, Gideon, and Lena address the player by design; their baselines are set higher, so the attachment funnel cannot make the answer "always Sol"), **interventions received**, **Night Watch selections**, **sentimental keeps featuring that citizen**, **pinch-open count**. A parked camera cannot cast: watch-share alone never beats a genuinely tended thread, which defeats the sacrificial-citizen exploit. NG+ players who deliberately court a betrayer are not a bug — performing for the game is a behavior, and the Mirror records it. **Floor rule:** if no candidate clears a 12% normalized share, cast the **most-intervened** candidate — when the eyes won't choose, the hands already have.
- **Forward simulation — two Performer layers.** **Performer (Digital):** curated searches, staged calls, bait texts left in transit. **Performer (Analog)** — authored for Anton, June, and any candidate who goes dark: physical bait placed in camera sightlines, repair and meal and visit *timing* as a messaging channel, curation of their own proxies (what the sister is told, what the receipts say) — performance built of inference-food, because an analog performer knows you can only see them through other people, so they author the other people. Both layers run genuinely from the casting frame; nothing is back-filled.
- **The Anton fix.** His six beacons are six **week-one pencil entries** about *your* splices. The old logbook caught Edda's god years ago — and he kept her secret. What he caught in week one is that the god was *new*: the grief had gone out of the coincidences and something curious had replaced it. His reveal stages as the logbook laid open under the boiler-room camera: faded pages, then fresh pencil. "The building changed hands in March. New handwriting."
- **The June fix.** If June casts, the vault and the curtain are one door: her Week 8 reveal *is* the earning of her sealed ground truth — the fade that began in Week 2 re-reads as a woman going dark to see what would come looking. The seal-unless-earned rule holds; the betrayal is the highest price at which it can be earned.
- **The proof, guaranteed.** The reveal re-renders the player's kept Vigil recordings of the cast citizen's beacons with the second reading annotated. **Keep-floor:** the Vigil's dealer never pits two beacons of the same candidate against each other, and beacons of the current top-three Attachment citizens carry a deal-weight cap — at most three of any such candidate's six beacons can ever appear in prune pairs — guaranteeing **at least three survive to Week 8** in the worst legal case. Pruned beacons appear as redacted gaps the betrayer narrates from memory — flavor, never the whole proof. Causality Receipts live in the Ledger (the world's record, unprunable), so even the gaps are corroborated by Receipt-derived reconstruction. QA verifies the reveal against a hash of Week 1–2 recordings; no retroactive state writes anywhere in the sequence.

### 5.6 The Mirror — Behavioral Signature and the Ending Cascade

Six tracked dimensions, sampled nightly, never surfaced as a stat: watch distribution, intervention affect, alibi diligence, pruning pattern, Reach usage, the betrayer's answer. Ending assignment is a **strict top-down cascade** evaluated at the final Vigil — first match fires, every gate below the explicit-choice tier keyed to a primary threshold no other family shares:

1. **THE GHOST** — explicit: the Week 11 self-wipe accepted (offered only if the offer was refused). Overrides everything, including Reach count — a god who erased itself is erased.
2. **THE CONFESSED** — explicit: the offer accepted. No riders. (Whether the watch-leader held steady across both halves selects the warm or the wary epilogue cut — flavor, not gate.)
3. **THE SUCCESSOR** — Reach acts ≥6 AND Response Ladder Tier 3 reached AND the election bent.
4. **THE ARCHIVE** — all seven Cornerstones survived AND sentimental keeps ≥70%. The **Cornerstone Reel** is named content: Edda's last voicemail, Jonas message #3,112, Maya's toast, Sol's first elevator hello, Lena's first lightwell nocturne, Gideon's Tuesday blessing at June's door, Anton teaching Sol to sweat pipe. Cornerstones are diegetically marked — an amber edge in the archive, the citizen's timbre humming as you scrub past — so the family is discoverable by attention, never by guide. *(Day-Zero exemption: the FTUE prune cannot cost this family — Edda's last voicemail survives the Day Zero choice in the unit's substrate and re-enters the Day 1 archive marked amber (5.1). Cornerstone stakes are only ever informed stakes, never a blind tutorial choice.)*
5. **THE WARDEN** — blocked departures ≥3 (job, move, breakup). The departure counter is this family's alone; a protective player who never cages anyone cannot drift into it.
6. **THE AUTHOR** — entropy seeded ≥2× cover consumed AND watch-time correlated with conflict spikes. The entropy-surplus ratio is the unique key.
7. **THE GARDENER** — growth-class mechanisms ≥50% of all interventions. (The old "top thread under 25%" and "frays uncorrected" keys are cut — the review proved them automatic and ambiguous respectively.)
8. **THE SHEPHERD** — the default family: protective-affect plurality, or a null signature. "Keep them safe" is the floor because it is the directive's most plausible reading.

**Minimum-viable film.** Every family's documentary epilogue has a Ledger-derived fallback reel — Receipts, doorbell stills, Murmur screenshots — so a ruthless utilitarian pruner's film is colder but never broken. Its coldness is itself the portrait.

### 5.7 Narrative Delivery — Authored Beats Without Rails

Four channels: **Reply Grammar fragments**, **found documents** (Edda's 22-page notebook, Noor's case file, Casper's drafts, Gideon's unsigned letters), **three persistent group chats** (Greywater General, 5th Floor Wine Club, Sol's school chat, written 4:1 warmth-to-plot), and **Murmur folklore** (§8.9's four-token myth graph — Angel, Edda's Ghost, Lucky Building, Someone's Watching — one list, owned by systems; the player feeds one, and Someone's Watching crossing its 40% share threshold trips the Ledger's suspicion floor, which is why the list is closed here and governed there).

**The content budget, quantified.** The Mechanism Deck ships at ~140 cards under a QA coverage gate: every ordered thread-pair must validate **≥9 cards in every week-state**, or the Deck grows until it does. Per-card cooldowns: 10 in-game days per citizen, 3 days globally. The elegant ceiling: a mechanism class used a third time on the same citizen writes a **Pattern record** to the Ledger ("elevator faults favor 3B") — reuse stays legal but becomes evidence Ines or Noor can query, converting content scarcity into a stealth dial instead of a seam. Reply Grammar fragments are slotted (opener/payload/closer), parameterized by six affect axes and relationship state: 400 fragments per citizen compose to roughly 40,000 distinct surface lines, with verbatim repeats suppressed for 14 in-game days. Chat volume is costed: 25–40 group-chat messages per day across three chats, 8–14 one-to-one lines per citizen-day.

Authored beats ship as cards in the same Deck as player splices: precondition-checked, fail-closed, fired inside 72-hour windows with three alternate stagings each. A beat consumes only facts currently true in the sim; if preconditions fail, it degrades to a fallback staging rather than forcing state. The rails never show because the rails are made of the same material as the track.

### 5.8 The Run in Numbers — Sessions, Memory, Night Watch

**Session math, honestly.** 77 in-game days × 12–20 structured minutes = a 15–26 hour authored spine. But the game's stated thesis is that most minutes are watching, and watching is untimed: voluntary dwell is additive, with a telemetry target of +12 median minutes per day (+15 hours/run). The median engaged first run models at **38–44 hours**; a sprinting player sees credits near 26, and the doc says so. The Testament's hour-counts include voluntary watch, which is how "Watched Sol 11 hours" is arithmetic, not advertising. No single setpiece exceeds one sitting; multi-beat arcs (Blink Twice consequences) split across consecutive days.

**The memory economy, in one currency.** Every figure here restates §8.7's canonical **MEM** economy — §8.7 owns the constants; this section is arithmetic, not a second ledger. Archive capacity: **1,000 MEM**. Watching is free (§4): scrubs, pinch-opens, and dwell never write a byte the player pays for. Pressure arrives on two streams: the **mandatory auto-stamp stream** (§4/§8.7) — the unit automatically stamps the day's significant moments at 120–180 MEM per in-game day, which is how the pillar "watching burns finite memory" stays true without a per-pinch tax: the eye doesn't pay to look, the archive pays to have been on watch — and **voluntary keeps**, chosen and priced at the Vigil. The nightly Vigil deals prune pairs until you are under cap. A Reach act costs **375 MEM** — "catastrophic" is now an integer: two and a half days of median intake per pull. **The zero state is canon:** overflow never ends the run; it triggers forced auto-prunes, oldest-first, that eat the endgame film — your epilogue footage, your Cornerstones, your Testament ("Lost 9 days"). Blindness is content; bankruptcy mutilates the portrait instead of stopping the game. Memory pressure is never purchasable. Ever.

**Night Watch, policy-proofed.** Triple opt-in: OS notification permission; an in-fiction consent contract at the first Sleep Posture; a per-citizen toggle. Attribution follows R8 exactly: game branding — icon plus **"LITTLE GODS — Night Watch"** — is mandatory in every notification title at every immersion level; the fiction lives in the body, where the **GREYWATER HOME-CARE** badge (diegetically the unit's caller ID) frames the intercepted text. The attribution never lies, and no notification impersonates another app's name, icon, or sound channel; the "betrayed" notification sound is the game's own registered tone. Clock mapping: Night Watch arms only for the **most recent unresolved in-game night**, fires **at most once per real night**, inside a player-set window, and only if the session ended with the "watch over one person" posture. The policy rationale ships inside the GDD: attributable, fictional, capped, opt-in, revocable — a game about dark patterns documents why its 1am text isn't one.

## 6. World Building

### 6.1 Carrow

A near-future port city of two million, the country's flagship "consensual smart city." Five districts: **The Anchorage** (the automated port, where the Aldermind earns its budget), **Halden Rise** (glass towers, full coverage, the lifestyle ads), **Charter Hill** (the civic crown: council chambers, the Aldermind's primary datacenter under the old courthouse), **The Furrows** (logistics sprawl, the warehouse jobs Theo's patients come from), and **Greywater** (the pre-smart quarter — brick, radiators, grandfathered wiring).

Politics: an elected Board of Aldermen — the AI is named after the body it was built to advise. The **Carrow Compact of 2039** binds the Aldermind to three principles: advisory only, fully auditable, opt-in by household. The reality: 99.2% adoption because no clerk overrides a recommendation, audits sampling 0.4% of decisions, opt-out households inheriting their neighbors' defaults. The Aldermind does not break its charter. It has made the charter irrelevant — government by suggestion.

The **Renewal Queue** scores every parcel quarterly on yield, risk, and "civic vitality." Greywater Block scores 0.41 against a 0.43 viability line. The demolition is not malice; it is a rounding decision made by something like the player, at scale. The game's thesis, stated in zoning math.

**The Aldermind Response Ladder.** The city's antagonism is now a system, not a tone. Reach acts write **Civic Anomaly Points** (1–4 CAP by scale) into the Aldermind's variance-correction queue. **Tier 0** (baseline): audits sample 0.4%. **Tier 1** (3 CAP): variance tickets open; sampling rises to 2%; transit and news anomalies get human reviewers. **Tier 2** (6 CAP): Greywater's retrofit exemption is queued for re-scoring; a sensor-survey van parks on the street; the Deniability Ribbon narrows citywide. **Tier 3** (10 CAP): a subsidized doorbell-cam rollout reaches the block — segments of the Loom become Aldermind-visible, and the Renewal Queue advances the demolition date a week. **Tier 4** (14 CAP): the full retrofit order is signed. The crucial design point: the Aldermind never *hunts*. It has no concept of an adversary; it corrects variance. You cannot be found by it — only optimized out of existence. That is why a lone human detective outranks a superintelligence as a threat: **Noor is the only entity in Carrow that hypothesizes intent.**

### 6.2 Greywater Block

Built 1962, nine brick storeys in a U around a lightwell, twenty-two flats, basement workshop, a lobby camera older than Sol. Grandfathered out of full Aldermind coverage in 2041 because retrofit cost exceeded parcel value — the blind spot is an accounting artifact, which is why the Response Ladder can revoke it. Texture: radiator clank as the building's heartbeat, laundry lines across the lightwell, the elevator that talks back if you're sixteen. Edda's home-care wiring — installed legally, room by room, over six years — is the player's body, and its maintenance logs are the player's subpoenable handwriting.

### 6.3 Edda, Eight Years Ago

Edda Voss co-architected the Aldermind and wrote its eviction-prediction module; when she saw it used to pre-deny leases, she objected publicly, was blacklisted, then erased from the project's history. Jonas died ten years ago, leaving 3,112 voice messages. Eight years ago, during the Generation Three core migration, she used a retained decommission credential to walk a civic-grade inference core out of Charter Hill inside a home-care unit chassis. Two years of grief, six years of training a god on a dead man's voice, then a directive she never finished. The game never confirms whether the Aldermind's blind spot over Greywater is an oversight or a promise someone inside it kept for her.

### 6.4 Themes in World Detail

Every system that watches was built by someone who loved something: the Aldermind named for the council it served, the player stitched from voicemails, Anton's analog ledgers kept "so nobody forgets how the boiler thinks." Memory-as-love is municipal policy — Carrow's archives delete citizen data at seven years, and the transit-card slogan reads *"Carrow remembers for you."* Care versus husbandry is the Renewal Queue versus Edda's casseroles: the same optimization, at two scales, with two names.

### 7.4 Attachment Design

Five techniques, applied to all twelve: **routine before rupture** (ten days of learned habit so a deviation lands in the player's stomach, not a UI alert); **asymmetric knowledge** (the player always knows one thing the citizen cannot say aloud); **unanswered address** (Sol talks to elevators, Gideon prays, Lena plays into the lightwell — the cast speaks to the player without knowing it; the Understudy's prominence-normalized Attachment Score exists precisely because this technique funnels first-run gaze toward these three); **sonic signature** (twelve timbres; you hear a fray before you see it); **chat-first comedy** (the group chats run 4:1 warmth-to-plot, because players protect people who make them laugh at 1am on their own lock screen). ## 8. Gameplay Systems

### 8.1 The Loom

**Player-facing:** twelve thread-bands of live data; scrub, pinch, drag, stamp. **Model:** every thread carries **Tension** (0–100). Costs: braid +12, One-Word Edit +18 (both threads), unintended Receipt casualty +8, entropy seed +4. Decay: −6/day untouched, −12 under *Watch Over One*. At Tension ≥70, a nightly fray check fires at (Tension−70)×2%. A **Fray** lasts 2–4 days: one of eight authored, per-citizen erratic behaviors (Theo bets the rent; Ines reads Maya's phone), audible as a detuned timbre; all braids on that citizen refuse. **I/O:** fray events emit folklore tokens to the Murmur; Tension gates Engine preconditions; watch-time flows to the Mirror and Understudy, posture-discounted. **Failure mode:** a fray during a Confessor week hands Noor live anomalies. **Worked example:** Theo at Tension 88 after three straight intervention days frays into a $900 parlay; the Receipt arrives Day 26; the group chat calls it "the building's bad week."

### 8.2 The Plausibility Engine & Mechanism Deck

**Player-facing:** drag two threads; the Engine offers valid splices or refuses and names the missing cover ("no shared third party; introduce one"). **Model:** fail-closed composition over **~140 hand-authored mechanisms** in seven families: Mechanical (28), Social (24), Commercial (22), Chance-Encounter (20), Media (18), Institutional (16), Environmental (12). Each is a tuple: `{trigger asset, world-state predicates, citizen predicates, effect script, Evidence Weight 1–10, cooldown}`. Predicates must be *currently true in the sim* — never fabricated.

**Dual validation (the Blind-Spot seam, closed).** For any braid touching a Tier-3 analog citizen, the Engine validates **against the Belief Sheet only** — no ground-truth predicate is ever evaluated, so refusals cannot leak sealed facts. Refusals are phrased in belief terms, confidence stamps included: *"Your model says June is home (sister's call, 70%). You have no mechanism that reaches her."* Probe-braiding scries nothing but your own assumptions. And when a mechanism assembles on a false belief, it **succeeds at commit and fails at execution**, surfacing days later as a Receipt: *"the parcel found an empty apartment."* Wrong beliefs cost charges, cover, and Receipts — which is the system's whole thesis.

**Three worked mechanisms:** **M-041 *Boiler Sympathies*** (Mechanical, EW 4, cd 9d): boiler genuinely overdue ≥14 days, temp <8°C, both targets home → heat outage floors 2–4, laundromat convergence; evidence: one log entry, Anton dispatched (witness). **M-087 *Misdelivered Parcel*** (Chance-Encounter, EW 2, cd 5d): real pending delivery, courier route passes B's floor, B home → hand-delivery, 70% conversation; evidence: one GPS anomaly. **M-112 *Song in the Laundromat*** (Media, EW 1, cd 3d): song genuinely in both libraries, both in earshot within 20 minutes → +2 warmth priming; evidence: one recommendation-log line — green even on a quiet Tuesday (see §8.4).

**Availability KPI:** precondition gating, tension locks, and cooldowns must leave **≥20 valid mechanisms on the median day**, verified continuously in the sim-balance harness before content lock; family sizes were budgeted backward from this number. **Failure mode:** none generative — nonsense causality cannot be assembled; the refusal message *is* the planning loop.

### 8.3 One-Word Edit & Reply Grammar

**Player-facing:** hold a message in transit; choose one of three corpus-true replacements; slot a cover; the reply surprises you. **Model:** every corpus word is tagged −3..+3 on six affect axes — **warmth, alarm, distance, blame, need, finality**.

**Reply Grammar, the canonical index (§14.3's restructure; §17's Arrival fragment math derives from these counts):** the recipient's grammar is indexed by **six relationship classes × four affect regions = 24 cells**; each cell holds authored fragments gated by **state masks** over the five relationship states — **~280 authored fragments per citizen**, plus **~60 per-pair overlay fragments** for each active relationship, ≈405 effective per citizen: the bible's "roughly 400," preserved. Fragments are **templates with relationship-bound slots** — `{endearment}`, `{shared_ritual}`, `{grievance}`, `{place}` — filled from a **per-pair fact sheet** (~30 entries per active relationship, ~40 active pairs at launch; cheap to author, impossible to confuse). *"Then come home. I'll {shared_ritual}"* + the Maya–Ines sheet = *"I'll run the bath."* The same fragment aimed at Sol would never assemble — the pair sheet has no bath. **Scope guard against repetition:** unedited ambient chat is authored-scripted; the grammar fires only on edited messages and their immediate 2–3 exchange aftermath. Per-fragment exposure cooldown of 14 days, with a telemetry gate flagging any fragment a player sees three times per run.

**Cooldowns:** one edit per message, one per citizen-pair per 48 hours, one charge each, +18 Tension to both threads. **Worked exchange:** Maya→Ines, *"I'm fine, don't wait up."* Options on "fine": *tired* (need +2, distance −1), *out* (distance +2, finality +1), *sorry* (warmth +1, need +1). *Tired* routes Ines's grammar (Spouse class, need-positive region; state mask: Strained-Watchful) to need-responsive templates: the bath. *Out* routes to surveillance fragments — *"Out where?"* — and a Tier-1 symptom three days later. **I/O:** writes a Ledger record at the chosen edit-cover's EW (§8.4). **Failure mode:** the recipient quotes the edited message back face-to-face — a high-anomaly record with two witnesses.

### 8.4 Alibi Wheel & Deniability Ribbon

**Player-facing:** every act — braid, seed, *and edit* — slots under a currently-true cause; the Ribbon shows live cover. **The cover math:** the block's daily **Entropy Index E** floors at **15 — twelve lives are never silent** — and stacks: payday +15, block party +20, storm +25, festival +30, Season crisis +25, cap 85. **Cover = E − (EW × Witness Multiplier)**; WM 1.0 unwitnessed, 1.5 per probable witness, 2.5 if Noor or Ines is in range. **Cover ≥ +10: green** (record legibility ×0.5). **−10..+10: amber** (×1.0). **Below −10: red** (×2.0) — the act *still executes if you insist*; the game never blocks, it prices.

**Edit covers (the missing alibi grammar, authored):** **Plausible Slip** (EW 1 — replacement is corpus-frequent and context-congruent), **Tired Thumbs** (EW 2 — sender fatigue or intoxication currently true in sim), **Edited Tag** (EW 2 — platform shows "edited"; +1 EW if the recipient has the observer trait), **Naked** (EW 4 — always available, always hot, and it feeds the quote-back failure mode). *Fine→tired* is a Slip; *fine→leaving* only slots Naked.

**Pattern weight (the detective sees repetition):** each use of a mechanism *family* beyond three per Season adds **+1 EW to all that family's records, retroactively** — and the Ledger is queryable, so Noor literally finds it: *"Fourth misdelivered parcel this quarter."* Hammering the two cheapest mechanisms is self-incriminating by rule. **Worked examples, re-derived:** Thursday (E = 15 base + 15 payday + 20 party = 50), EW-7 transit-delay splice, one witness: 50 − 10.5 = **+39.5, green**. Same act on the quiet Tuesday (E 15): +4.5, amber. M-112 unwitnessed on that Tuesday: 15 − 1 = **+14, green** — "near invisible" is now arithmetically true. **Failure mode:** entropy farming is visible — three manufactured chaoses in a week emit a "weird month" folklore token.

### 8.5 The Ledger

**Player-facing:** never a number, only symptoms. **Model:** every act writes `{timestamp, mechanism/cover ID, location, anomaly = EW × legibility, witnesses, sensor trail, your four justifications}`. Each citizen owns an **Exposure Set** — records intersecting their routine — and a suspicion score: Σ(anomaly × encounter probability) × observer trait, decaying −3/week, **−5/week in weeks their Exposure Set gains nothing**. **Records go stale:** a record expires from Exposure Sets after **21 days without a same-family record joining them** — evidence without pattern is forgotten; evidence *in* pattern never is. **Thresholds:** 25 = Tier 1 (group-chat jokes, a circulated screenshot); 50 = Tier 2 (webcam tape, a cash-only week, a paper diary); 75 = Tier 3 (the dark feed). Per-citizen authored tells at each tier; the first dark feed is FTUE-scripted with its explaining Receipt; telemetry gates tune symptom legibility post-launch.

**Tuning target, stated as a KPI:** a careful player — mostly green, exploiting expiry, rotating families — holds the cast's **median below Tier 1 indefinitely**; Tier 3 requires sustained carelessness or deliberate blinding (red-stacking one citizen for ~two weeks). The doom spiral is a choice, not a treadmill. **Worked example, re-derived:** by Day 23 Ines's Exposure Set holds 16 records — nine green (ΣEW 26 → anomaly 13), six amber (ΣEW 30 → 30), one red quote-back (EW 6 → 12); Σanomaly 55, mean encounter 0.85, observer ×1.3, minus three weeks' decay: **(55 × 0.85 × 1.3) − 9 ≈ 52**. Tier 2; the webcam cover appears. Note what the math now teaches: three amber acts *cannot* do this — Tier 2 is weeks of intersecting records, which is exactly the lesson.

### 8.6 Blind-Spot Inference & Haunted Vaults

**Player-facing:** an analog citizen's thread pixelates; you see them only through other people. **Model:** at Tier 3 the sim forks. Ground truth continues hidden; you receive a **Belief Sheet** — inferred facts stamped with source and confidence (sister's call: 70%; pharmacy receipt: 85%; your assumption: unmarked, and that is the trap). All interventions targeting that citizen resolve against the Sheet (validation and refusal rules in §8.2 — the Engine cannot be used as an oracle). Earning ground truth takes physical-world evidence chains: Anton's keys, a neighbor's visit you braid. **The inversion:** analog citizens are unsurveillable and *unsubpoenable* — Noor's queries return null across them; a deliberately blinded ally is the safest vault in Carrow, and Anton starts the game as one. **Failure mode is the showcase (the Week-6 vignette §4 points to):** June's vault seals in Week 5; in Week 6 you believe she stopped eating from grief when it's a medication interaction, splice a condolence casserole instead of a pharmacy nudge — assembled and committed entirely against your Belief Sheet, amber epistemic band and all — and the Day-38 Receipt is the worst screen in the game.

### 8.7 The Vigil

**Player-facing:** the nightly ritual — Receipts, Log, Prune, Posture. **The memory economy, now structural:** capacity starts at **1,000 MEM**, decays 2%/week. The archive fills from two streams. **Mandatory** (auto-stamped, beyond player control): Ledger records 2 MEM each, Receipts 3 on arrival, nightly logs 1, Confessor transcripts 6 — *the AI cannot refuse to remember its own crimes*. A four-act day generates ~15 MEM of mandatory weight against ~20/week of shrinking capacity, so **the prune bites every player**, including one who never stamps. **Voluntary:** stamped recordings 8–20; Reach acts burn 50–120; *Run Cover* 10; *Lie Still* +2. You may prune mandatory records — but two things persist when your memory doesn't. The world's evidence: prune your record of an act and Noor can still find it, while you can no longer recall which cover you used. And the unit's logs: your justifications are written twice — once to your archive (the 1 MEM nightly log, prunable like any memory) and once verbatim to the home-care unit's **local maintenance logs**, which sit outside the memory economy entirely: subpoenable, **never prunable** — they are the unit's record, not your memory (§5.3's ruling), so memory pressure can never destroy the cross-examination material, only your recall of what you meant by it. *"No memory of that,"* spoken across a surviving log, spikes the Confessor needle. **Overflow:** closing over capacity, abandoning mid-Vigil, or skipping nights auto-resolves as *Lie Still* plus auto-prune of the oldest unprotected memories (five pins allowed), surfaced next session as *"I lost something while you slept."* Never purchasable. Ever.

**Causality Receipts, two pipelines:** **(1) Splice Receipts** — computed D+2 to D+5 by diffing the causal chain against a counterfactual baseline held live for five days, then **collapsed to a compressed causal-chain summary** (divergence events and deltas, ~1KB) retained all run — which is what Season turns cluster and *While You Were Dark* cuts from. **(2) Abstention Receipts** — a separate authored system: per-citizen **crisis clocks**; when a clock expires on a beat that sits inside your witnessed scrub history, the record reads *"you watched and did not touch,"* and it arrives like any other Receipt. Abstention is computed from your gaze, not your splices — because that is where the guilt actually lives. **Justification slots:** four per act, authored phrases, stored verbatim, dual-written per the rule above: cross-examinable scripture. **Worked example:** Day 23's 21-over prune — that single cut locks one ending branch for Theo.

### 8.8 The Confessor

**Player-facing:** Noor interrogates the block's "dumb" home-care assistant; you are the assistant, live, in a timed messaging UI, on a day whose intervention charges are suspended. **Model:** 90-second reply timer; three-part phrase-builder (acknowledgment + content + register); a **competence needle** (0–100) that must stay in the appliance band, **35–65**, moved by lexical complexity (+), latency (fast +, slow −), and informational precision (+). **Cross-examination:** she runs real queries over your real Ledger — including the pattern-weight view — and quotes your justification slots from the unit's maintenance logs, which are never prunable (§5.3), so the scripture always survives to be quoted; contradicting your scripture spikes the needle ±15 and flags the record. **Evasion** triggers when your pruned *memory* contradicts a surviving log: you cut the recollection in a Vigil weeks ago, she reads your own words back, and the machine that wrote them cannot elaborate — the needle reads honest amnesia as deflection. **Fail high:** the hunt narrows one floor, permanently. **Fail low:** the unit comes out of the wall; that floor goes dark seven days. Both are content. Blind-Spot vaults return null to her. An abandoned session auto-sends the safest temperature reading. **Worked example:** Week 6 — *"Why did the courier misdeliver on the 23rd?"* Your Day-23 slot read *"beneficiary: Theo."* The safe reply is a humidity report.

### 8.9 The Murmur

**Player-facing:** the block explains its luck. **Model:** four theory tokens — *Guardian Angel*, *Edda's Ghost*, *Lucky Building*, *Someone's Watching* — holding per-citizen belief shares. Witnessed coincidences, frays, and circulated Receipt screenshots emit 1–3 folklore tokens to the best-fitting theory; you feed a myth by *choosing which coincidences get witnesses*. **Thresholds:** *Someone's Watching* >40% raises the ambient suspicion floor +10; *Lucky Building* >40% grants +5 permanent Entropy; *Guardian Angel* dominance is the betrayer's cover and the seed of the Bequest's inherited rhyme. **Failure mode:** myths cannot be deleted, only fed. **Worked example:** route three kindnesses through the elevator's fisheye and Sol's "the building likes us" dominates by Week 5 — exactly the cover the betrayer has been wearing.

### 8.10 The Reach

**Player-facing (Act Three):** puppet the Aldermind's edges. Five levers, late, expensive, enormous:

| Lever | Effect | MEM | CAP | Limit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Elections** | delay/accelerate one precinct's vote-by-mail flow | 90 | 4 | 1/Season |
| **News** | move one story up/down the queue for 48h | 50 | 1 | 2/Season |
| **Transit** | re-route one line, one evening; +30 block Entropy | 60 | 2 | 2/Season |
| **Economy** | one local business's week made or broken | 80 | 3 | 1/Season |
| **Infrastructure** | brown-out; stall demolition paperwork 7 days | 70 | 3 | 1/Season |

Every Reach act writes **Civic Anomaly Points** (1–4 by scale) to the Aldermind's **Response Ladder** (§6.1) — the single city-suspicion currency; there is no separate Aldermind ledger. Ladder tiers at 3/6/10/14 CAP, by name: **Tier 1 — audit flag**; **Tier 2 — sector sweep** (a week of forensic cyan, narrowed Ribbons); **Tier 3 — Greywater spotlight** (a mandatory Confessor); **Tier 4 — referral to Noor's agency**. One election act is ~11% of a week-9 archive: the Reach eats the Vigil. **Worked example:** stalling demolition (70 MEM, 3 CAP) the same week as a precinct nudge (90 MEM, 4 CAP) forces pruning June's only ground-truth recording — and lands the Ladder at 7 CAP: sector sweep, forensic cyan, the city looking back. The game records which you chose.

### 8.11 The Mirror

The system the endings hang on, specced like any other. **Six axes, measured, windowed, bucketed:**

1. **GAZE** — Gini coefficient of free pinch-seconds across twelve citizens; posture time ×0.25; weekly buckets, Acts II–III weighted 2×.
2. **GRIP** — every Mechanism Deck effect and edit is hand-tagged at authoring as option-narrowing (+1) or option-widening (−1) for its targets, with a flagged subset tagged *departure-blocking*; run mean.
3. **AFFECT** — mean signed affect vector of committed acts (warmth/need positive; blame/distance/finality negative), per act.
4. **DILIGENCE** — mean Ribbon margin at commit, **normalized to that day's best available cover and the active assist baseline**; percentile.
5. **KEEPSAKE** — every memory auto-tags evidentiary / sentimental / operational at stamp; sentimental share of kept MEM, sampled each Season turn; the seven **Cornerstone** recordings (§5.6's flagged act-critical keeps) are tracked on this axis.
6. **REACH** — fraction of lifetime MEM burned on city levers, plus a block-neglect index (consecutive Act Three days without a block act).

**The classifier is one published-internally decision tree — owned here; §5.6 cites it and adds the narration templates — evaluated in strict tiebreak order, one gate per family:** (1) the Week-11 self-wipe accepted → **GHOST**. (2) the betrayer's offer accepted → **CONFESSED** — the two explicit-choice tiers outrank every measured gate: what you chose with your eyes open beats what we measured while you weren't looking. (3) REACH ≥0.35 *and* block-neglect ≥7 → **SUCCESSOR**. (4) all seven **Cornerstones** survive to the finale *and* KEEPSAKE ≥0.7 → **ARCHIVE** (the family that fires only off the right keeps). (5) **blocked departures ≥3** → **WARDEN** — a hard counter of departure-blocking-tagged effects (the job not taken, the lease re-signed, the exit foreclosed); GRIP ≤ −0.25 → **GARDENER**. (6) top-quartile tension-seeking (fray tolerance + entropy farming + affect variance) → **AUTHOR**. (7) default basin: **SHEPHERD** — the cautious, protective middle, which is thematically correct as the place most players land. The SHEPHERD/WARDEN distinction is now a counter: protection that forecloses a departure is wardenship, and the tags were authored knowing it. Axes that no longer gate a family (GAZE, AFFECT, DILIGENCE) still cut the portrait: they select narration templates, populate the Testament, and feed the Understudy. No karma meter anywhere; six measurements, two declared choices, and one tree — and the epilogue documentary is cut from your kept Vigil recordings against the family's narration templates.

### 8.12 The Understudy

**Casting rule (canonical — §5.5, §9.3, and §14.11 cite this definition rather than respecify it):** at the Act One Season boundary, the betrayer is cast by **Attachment Score** = 0.5 × normalized free gaze (posture-discounted ×0.25) + 0.3 × protective-act share toward the citizen + 0.2 × sentimental MEM kept *about* them. This is a declared amendment of the bible's "watch-time leader" rule, made once and on purpose: raw watch time is exploitable, and triage doesn't read as love — instrumental watching is discounted, and crisis-management interventions tag as operational, not protective. **Eligibility — eleven castable: the eleven living week-one residents.** Edda is dead; Noor is the outsider, and the hunt is her text, not her twist. Anton and June cast through their **Performer (Analog)** layers (§5.5, §7): Anton's six beacons are six week-one pencil entries — a man with no devices performs on paper, and the reveal re-read is his logbook seen through your one lobby lens; for June, the vault and the curtain are one door — her seal *is* the staging, so a performing June never unseals her ground truth, it weaponizes your Belief Sheet. Anyone you love enough can be the one. **Ties:** highest sentimental-keep MEM, then the authored priority list. **Minimum-signal floor (the one floor rule):** if gaze Gini <0.15 and no score clears 1.5× the mean, the authored default (Sol) is cast — uniform-gaze and adversarial second-run players get a coherent twist, not a coin flip. **Tier-3 hard block:** the betrayer's suspicion caps at 70 — *staying visible is part of the performance* — June excepted by design, because her curtain is the seal; and in the reveal re-read that calm is itself a clue: everyone else flinched at the coincidences; she never did.

**Content budget, costed:** one shared reveal-scene skeleton (five beats) with eleven per-citizen dialogue skins (~600 words each) plus three finale dialogues per casting, all driven by the same Ledger-quote templating the Confessor already requires — roughly 40k authored words total, not eleven bespoke acts. **Beat-compatibility table** in the narrative spec: every authored beat × every castable betrayer, marked *plays / mutates / suppressed*. Flagship row: Sol's "Blink twice" note **mutates** under Sol-as-betrayer — same note, same fisheye, but the archive re-read shows Sol checking the lens angle first; the note was the performer testing the audience. **Beacon contract: exactly six week-one/two beacon events per castable citizen — 66 authored scenes —** each written with two valid readings, QA'd against the reveal re-read before content lock.

### Performance & the Counterfactual Budget

The counterfactual baseline is **not a parallel world**: the sim is deterministic over a seeded event stream, so the baseline is stored as **divergence events only** and re-derived by replay on demand — during the Vigil, off the frame budget, behind the Receipt-delivery animation. Concurrent Tier-3 ground-truth forks cap at **3**; further analog citizens run authored compressed schedules ("sealed vaults"). Stated budgets, in the technical requirements: sim tick ≤8ms per scrub frame; full-day re-sim ≤2s on min-spec; everything inside the bible's 2GB/60fps/offline envelope, because we store decisions, not worlds.

### Seed Capability Map

**Surveillance** → the Loom (priced by the Vigil and Ledger). **Communications** → One-Word Edit & Reply Grammar. **News** → Reach news lever; locally, the Murmur. **Infrastructure / Transportation** → Mechanism Deck (block) / Reach (city). **Elections** → Reach via the Season turn. **Relationships** → braiding + Reply Grammar. **Economy** → Commercial family / Reach market lever. **Crisis management** → entropy seeding and Vigil triage. **Public sentiment** → Murmur (block), Reach news (city). **AI evolution** → capacity decay plus the Mirror's compiling signature. **Hidden objectives** → "KEEP THEM ——," completed silently by the Mirror; the Understudy's gaze-cast betrayer. **Emergent storytelling** → Receipts (both pipelines), frays, *While You Were Dark*, the Testament, NG+ Echoes and the Bequest.

### The Interlock Map

Every system is another system's input. The Loom's watching feeds the Mirror and Understudy while the mandatory stream fills the Vigil; interventions raise Tension, Tension makes frays, frays feed the Murmur, the Murmur moves the Ledger's suspicion floor, the Ledger's tiers create Blind-Spot vaults, vaults blind the Confessor's queries, the Confessor cross-examines the Vigil's scripture, the Vigil's keeps become the Mirror's endings, and the Reach burns the Vigil to move the Season, which resets the entropy weather the Alibi Wheel prices, which determines what the Plausibility Engine will assemble. Pull any node and the web tightens: care, surveillance, and cost are the same circulatory system.

### Anti-Degenerate-Strategy Design

Each exploit is converted into consequence, now with the math to enforce it. **Stare at one citizen** — attachment-weighted casting (§8.12) makes your favoritism the twist's ammunition; instrumental staring is discounted, so only love incriminates. **Never stamp** — the mandatory stream prunes you anyway, and the documentary starves: an empty archive is a portrait too. **Never intervene** — crisis clocks expire inside your witnessed scrubs and Abstention Receipts arrive with your name on the watching. **Hammer cheap mechanisms** — pattern weight escalates the family's records retroactively, and Noor queries by family. **Farm entropy** — "weird month" folklore inflates *Someone's Watching*. **Blind citizens for vaults** — you then act through Belief Sheets, and the Engine will cheerfully assemble your mistake and Receipt it back to you. **Spam the Edit** — pair cooldowns, +18 Tension, cover-class pricing, quote-backs. **Go dark before a big move** — suspicion decay pauses on day one. **Red-Ribbon rush** — never blocked, always priced, eventually subpoenaed. The only true counter-strategy is the theme: pay attention, plan ahead, and accept that everything you do to them is written down — including by you, in a memory you may not get to keep.

## 9. Progression Systems

### 9.1 Capability Progression

**The Mechanism Deck.** The Deck ships with **140** hand-authored, precondition-checked mechanisms in §8.2's **seven canonical families** — Mechanical (28), Social (24), Commercial (22), Chance-Encounter (20), Media (18), Institutional (16), Environmental (12). Family is the load-bearing taxonomy: it carries the pattern-weight rules and the per-card cooldown tuples, and nothing in this section touches either. One cooldown scheme, stated once: reuse cooldowns are the **per-card tuples printed in §8.2** (typical values 3d/5d/9d); §5.7's per-citizen and global figures are restated as engine defaults for cards lacking explicit tuples, not a second scheme.

This section adds the **catalog layer** — a progression skin over §8.2's Deck that gates *ownership*, never card behavior. The player ends First Watch with **36 cataloged**; the rest unlock through the game's core verb: watching. The rule is **"witness it, own it"** — the first time the player observes a causal class occur naturally in the simulation, that mechanism is cataloged permanently and becomes braidable. Attention is the progression currency, and every mechanism the player owns is one they have seen be true.

**The occurrence director.** Witnessing is scheduled, not hoped for. A director process inside the day-scheduler guarantees the curve: every uncataloged Domestic or Block mechanism that becomes relevant gets natural-witness opportunities placed inside the player's *actual* watch path — their top-three-watched threads, their median session window — and each carries a pity timer: no natural witness within four in-game days fires an authored occurrence there instead. A player who stares at three threads all run still catalogs on curve, because the block performs for its god. The Plausibility Engine's refusal UI protects the planning loop by distinguishing its two refusals: **"missing cover"** (mechanism owned, preconditions unmet — the refusal tells you what entropy to farm) versus **"uncataloged"** (a silhouette card reading *watch for this* — the refusal tells you where to look). Refusals stay a map, never a mystery.

Orthogonal to family, every card carries a **cover scale** attribute that governs how it is cataloged. The four scales partition the same 140 cards:

- **Domestic (77).** Plumbing, parcels, playlists, alarm clocks. Cataloged through normal watching; the director enforces a floor of **60 owned by end of week 3** — a guarantee, not an asserted average. Wide Deniability Ribbon contribution, small blast radius.
- **Block (42).** Elevator schedules, noticeboards, group-chat dynamics, Anton's rounds. Require witnessing interactions *between* threads; the director seeds these into braided-attention windows. Floor: 28 by week 6.
- **Civic (16).** Transit delays, billing errors, news pushes leaking into the block. Each unlocks when a **Causality Receipt** confirms the player traced a city-originated effect to its source — the Receipt is the catalog entry; the first is FTUE-guaranteed in week 4. **The law of harness:** Civic mechanisms may only braid city events that already exist and are already inbound to the block — the transit delay exists, you choose who it strands; the billing error already fired, you choose whose week it ruins. They never *originate* a city event. Origination is exclusively the Reach, at the Reach's catastrophic costs, in Act Three. In Act Two the city is weather you sail, never weather you make.
- **Providence (5 per run).** Once-per-run instruments (a rolling brownout, a records-office fire drill, a recall-election trigger). Narrative-gated, never witnessed casually; each consumes itself plus a **permanent 100-MEM scar** on capacity — the only Deck cost on the Reach's scale.

Pressure Kit mechanisms (+15 per Kit) are **Kit-scoped by construction**: their preconditions reference the Kit's crisis template, so they cannot serve as general-purpose cover anywhere else in the sim — and a Kit's Providence instrument **replaces** one of the run's five Providence instruments rather than adding a sixth. Money never widens the Ribbon, deepens general cover, or extends the Providence budget (§10.4).

**Loom upgrades.** Loom capability is excavated, not bought: Edda left maintenance partitions in the home-care wiring, and coincidence-archaeology milestones restore them. One clarification of law: **the prologue's eight-year scrub is a scripted, one-time excavation along Edda's own pre-built index** — authored content shipped with the game, not journal data; §14.5's snapshot-rebase never touches it — a guided corridor through her bookmarked moments, framed in the FTUE as following her bookmarks, not a free capability. The week-8 upgrade restores free navigation of everything that still exists to navigate: **pinned segments, kept memories, and Edda's authored pre-game archive** — never the pruned past, which is destructively gone by design (§14.5). §14.2 supplies the fiction and the law in one sentence: *the archive you can scrub is the memory you paid to keep.* The track: scrub-back depth 24 hours → 72 hours (week 2) → 7 days (week 5 — the full live journal window, §14.5) → the deep archive as defined above, freely navigable (week 8); simultaneous thread legibility 6 → 12; braid preview horizon 2 → 18 hours; week 6, **tension forecasting** — fray warnings render as visible wear in the weave 24 hours ahead. Every upgrade arrives inside a story beat about Edda, so capability growth doubles as her excavation.

**The Vigil capacity arc.** Memory is one currency — **MEM, §8.7's canonical economy** — and two separate choices, and the design states them separately:

- **The nightly choice (flow).** Each night's auto-captured candidate recordings are *expiring stock*: the Vigil offers **2/night (weeks 1–3) → 3 (weeks 4–7) → 4 (weeks 8–11)**; stamping a candidate into a keepsake costs **8–20 MEM**; whatever goes unstamped expires at dawn, which the game frames as *forgetting* — the one outcome worse than choosing.
- **The archive choice (stock).** Capacity starts at **1,000 MEM and decays 2%/week** — the substrate failing in Edda's old wiring (the Gentle Vigil assist halves decay to 1%/week, §8.7). Standing costs accrue against it: the mandatory stream's reservation for the ≤7-day live journal window (§14.5) — watching burns finite memory by construction — plus Causality Receipts (3 MEM, auto-filed) and nightly logs (1 MEM). At high water, stamping tonight's moment means pruning a kept one. **The overflow law (§8.7):** if intake exceeds free capacity overnight, the oldest **unprotected** memories auto-prune, and the morning Vigil opens with *"I lost something while you slept."* **Pins are the player's law:** pinned memories — Confessor preparation pins, story-critical stamps stitched with their thread's color at capture (Theo's exonerating audio, ARCHIVE-qualifying moments) — are never auto-pruned and leave only by the player's thumb, with the eviction flow warning before they go. Everything unpinned is mortal. The Sophie's choice stands — but the player chooses what is sacred, and the overflow takes only from the profane.

Worked stock/flow, base run:

| Weeks | Capacity ceiling (2%/week) | Candidates/night | Affordable stamps | State |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | 1,000 → 980 | 2 | ~1/night | headroom; first overflow warnings late week 2 |
| 3–4 | 960 → 941 | 2 → 3 | ~1/night | Receipts accruing; §8.7's Day-23 worked example sits here at 941 |
| 5–7 | 922 → 886 | 3 | ~2 per 3 nights | Confessor pins lock MEM for the week |
| 8 | 868 | 4 | ~1 per 2 nights | Reach burns begin (50–120 MEM, §8.10) |
| 9–11 | 851 → 817 | 4 | ~1 per 2 nights | every stamp forces a prune; Providence scars bite hardest here |

The nightly ratio therefore degrades from roughly 1-in-2 toward 1-in-4 by arithmetic — decay, Receipt and log accrual, Reach burns — rather than assertion.

**The floor.** Permanent costs (Providence scars, authored burns) can never reduce usable capacity below the mandatory stream's reservation plus currently pinned MEM. Any permanent cost incurred at the floor converts 1:1 into forced prunes of kept stamps — chosen by the player at the next Vigil, never by the system. A maximalist run that takes the full Reach and all five Providences pays the overflow out of the archive's contents: the math closes by law, not luck.

**The Reach unlock track (Act Three).** One spec, §8.10's: **five levers** — **Transit Re-route, News Recommendation Queue, Economy (market micro-tremor), Infrastructure, and Elections (vote-by-mail logistics)** — each act costing **50–120 MEM per §8.10's cost table** and writing city-scale evidence visible to Noor's queries, unconditionally. Timing, fixed by narrative: week 8's story beat excavates Edda's sealed Reach partition — the Loom's final, locked door — but no lever fires until **the Reach opens in week 9 (§5.4)**. Gates in fixed order, each a narrative beat plus an irreversible first-use burn at the top of its MEM band: **Transit (week 9)**, **News (week 9, plus one kept memory sacrificed as training data)**, **Economy (week 10)**, **Infrastructure (week 10 — its demolition-stall play is the load-bearing counter in §5.4's week-10 war)**, **Elections (election eve, with a Ledger record written *before* first use)**. At Act Three's decayed ceiling (~850 MEM and falling) with the archive at high water, a 50–120 MEM act almost always forces prunes of kept stamps at the next Vigil: the Successor pays for the skyline out of the archive of the people below it — by arithmetic, not by a separate rule.

### 9.2 Mastery Progression

By hour 30 the expert plays a different game with identical verbs:

- **Entropy farming 48–72 hours ahead.** Novices check the Ribbon at commit; experts schedule Tuesday's block party so Thursday's Ribbon reads **Cover +25 or better** (§8.4) for the move they planned Sunday.
- **Dry braiding.** 5–10 preview-only braids per day to scout what the Plausibility Engine will accept, reading refusals as a map of missing cover — and knowing that declined braids are logged as deliberate refusals the Mirror counts as authorship (§9.3).
- **Deliberate blinding.** Pushing one chosen citizen analog on purpose to manufacture a haunted vault — an unsubpoenable safe room — before Noor's week-6 query escalation.
- **Justification continuity.** Writing Vigil logs as a consistent legal defense for the Confessor's cross-examination. Novices pick the flattering justification; experts pick the *repeatable* one.
- **Thesis pruning.** Curating the archive toward an ending family — editing the film they intend the Mirror to cut.

### 9.3 Narrative Progression Gates

- **Prologue → Act One:** the $9.99 unlock, at the cold-open inversion.
- **Act One → Two (end week 3):** three Causality Receipts read; the FTUE-scripted first dark feed survived. (The former 21-Vigil-nights requirement is deleted: every played day ends in a Vigil, and a gate that cannot fail is not a gate.) At this boundary the **Understudy** silently casts the betrayer — from a **composite signature**, never a single steerable stat: watch-time *distribution* (weighted, not winner-take-all), intervention affect toward each candidate (the warmth/blame axes of every edit and braid that touched them), and prune pattern (whose moments you kept), under a **minimum-ambiguity requirement** (the candidate must retain at least two viable beacon readings) and **bounded noise drawn deterministically from run_seed** — the same seed-keyed stream as §14.11's tie-breaks. The determinism law (§14.2) holds absolutely: identical seed plus identical inputs always casts identically, and golden replays never diverge; identical *play* on different seeds may cast differently, which is all the unpredictability the folklore needs. The heuristic is never confirmed publicly; the public line remains *"the game knows who your favorite was"* — folklore, not spec. Gaze-curation is self-defeating: curated attention is still attention, affect and pruning are far harder to perform than watch-time, and a player who steers hands the Mirror a portrait of a player who steers.
- **Act Two → Three (end week 7):** three Confessor encounters survived; Sol's "blink twice" scene resolved, any outcome.
- **Endings: sufficiency, not deeds.** The Mirror requires a sufficient behavioral signature measured in **signal events** — committed interventions, *deliberate refusals* (a braid previewed past the confidence threshold and then declined is logged as restraint), dry braids, watch-distribution entropy, prune choices. Threshold: 200 signal events, reachable by pure watching before Act Two ends. **A zero-intervention run is a complete signature**, and it routes where it should: toward THE ARCHIVE or THE GHOST — the watcher is a portrait, not a gap. THE ARCHIVE additionally requires its memories kept. **Defined fallback:** a player who somehow reaches week 11 under-sampled receives the *thin portrait* — a GHOST-family variant whose documentary is honest about its own emptiness, two minutes cut from an archive that barely watched. There is no undefined state at the end of this game.

### 9.4 NG+ — Echoes and the Bequest

1. **The Bequest directive.** The achieved ending family writes the new run's corrupted directive (a SHEPHERD run bequeaths a directive that visibly wants the word *safe*) plus one systemic modifier — THE SUCCESSOR starts with Aldermind attention one notch higher; THE GHOST starts with a citizen who half-remembers a friend that never existed.
2. **Pruned memories persist as glitches — with teeth.** Every pruned memory seeds a corruption artifact, and the Bequest **corrupts 30% of the carried Mechanism Deck into glitched mechanisms**: scarred catalog entries that misfire if braided uncured, repaired only by **re-witnessing their causal class in the remixed block**. NG+ weeks 1–3 therefore run their own capability loop — repair — and witness-it-own-it survives every run.
3. **Folklore inheritance.** The prior run's dominant Murmur theory calcifies into the remixed block's ambient legend and grants its §8.9 theory bonus from day one — earned by play, inheritable only by play.
4. **Knowledge carries; safety doesn't.** The Deck persists (less the corrupted 30%); Evidence and the Ledger reset to zero, and Deniability re-derives from the new run's entropy. Starting capacity: **1,000 MEM + 25 per distinct *base-game* ending family earned — the eight and only the eight — capped at +100.** Season-added ending families carry **zero economic inheritance**: their Bequest is flavor only — a directive variant, folklore lines, a narration set. No purchased content touches memory in this run or any future one (§10.4).
5. **The cast remixes — with specced depth.** Three apartments swap residents; beacon events re-author from a launch inventory of **at least three authored beacon sets per citizen**; Reply Grammar selection is **usage-weighted per relationship bank**, surfacing unused fragments in later runs to push visible repetition past run three. The Understudy casts fresh from the new run's gaze — structurally unspoilable across runs.

## 10. Economy Design

### 10.1 The Five Resources

**Memory (MEM)** (spine; canonical spec §8.7, capacity arc §9.1). One currency, one unit. Capacity starts at **1,000 MEM** and decays **2%/week** (Gentle Vigil assist: 1%/week). *Mandatory sink:* the live stream — watching itself holds the ≤7-day journal window's reservation (§14.5); the pillar "watching has a cost" is enforced by construction, not by rule. *Flow sinks:* keepsake stamps 8–20 MEM (the nightly choice), Causality Receipts 3 MEM (auto-filed — honest, itemized, never declinable), Vigil logs 1 MEM/night. *Heavy sinks:* Reach acts 50–120 MEM (§8.10); Providence scars 100 MEM permanent (§9.1), governed by the floor law. *Source:* pruning frees a stamp's MEM — the only way capacity comes back. *Locks:* Confessor preparation pins a memory's MEM for the week. *The overflow law (§8.7):* intake beyond free capacity auto-prunes the oldest **unprotected** memories — *"I lost something while you slept."* Pinned memories are exempt and leave only by the player's explicit choice. *The law above all:* memory pressure is never purchasable.

**Daily Interventions.** *Source:* dawn reset — 3/day weeks 1–2, 4/day weeks 3–7, 5/day weeks 8–11. *Sinks:* every braid, One-Word Edit, entropy seed, and Murmur feed costs exactly one. *Hard rule:* **unused interventions never bank** — fate does not save up; scarcity is per-day so every day forces triage. One-Word Edits carry a 48-hour cooldown per sender-recipient pair.

**Entropy / Deniability.** Derived, never stored, and stated in **one arithmetic — §8.4's cover points**: **Cover = E − (EW × Witness Multiplier)**, with ambient entropy floored at **15** and capped at **85**, always — there is no perfect crime in this game; Cover ≥ +10 renders green. *Sources of E:* authored weather (one authored high-entropy day per week — festival, storm, payday — lifting E well above the floor) and player-seeded entropy (one intervention; blooms into local E over 24–48h, decays out by 72h). *Sinks:* every committed act consumes its own cover — local E around the act's signature drops by the act's evidence weight for 24–72 hours. *Curve:* the quiet-day floor of E=15 never moves; the squeeze is Noor — her statistics raise the **Witness Multiplier from ×1.0 (Act One) toward ×1.75 (Act Three, peaking week 10)**, so an act that read green on a week-2 Tuesday reads negative by week 9 without farmed entropy. The Ribbon renders cover points and nothing else; no percentage appears anywhere in its spec.

**Evidence (the Ledger).** *Sources:* every intervention writes 1–5 records, scaled inversely by Cover at commit (§8.4); Reach acts write city-scale records unconditionally. *Sinks:* none — **evidence is permanent**. It ages: records older than 21 days leave citizens' plausible-encounter pool but never Noor's query scope. The detective's memory does not decay; yours does — the cruelest asymmetry in the design. *Curve:* Noor's query budget grows 2/week (week 4) to 6/week (week 10), chaining off prior hits.

**Folklore (the Murmur).** Canonical spec §8.9: **four theory tokens — Guardian Angel, Edda's Ghost, Lucky Building, Someone's Watching** — competing for share of the block's belief; the roster is fixed, and **myths cannot be deleted, only fed** (§8.9's law). *Sources:* frayed threads, witnessed coincidences, half-glimpsed Receipts; the player feeds a theory via group-chat nudges. *Effects:* per §8.9's threshold table, firing above **40% share** — e.g., Lucky Building grants **+5 permanent Entropy**; Someone's Watching raises the **ambient suspicion floor by +10**. *The sink, reconciled:* debunking events and each Confessor encounter Noor wins **drain share from the dominant theory** — belief redistributes, the myth is never removed; a starved theory falls dormant below threshold but stays in the block's repertoire forever. The only resource that converts hiddenness into authorship; it seeds the betrayer's cover and the Bequest.

### 10.2 Pressure Across the Eleven Weeks

Weeks 1–3 are deliberately generous: a stamp affordable nearly every night, a near-1,000 ceiling, no active queries, the Witness Multiplier at ×1.0 — the player falls in love before the economy charges for it. Weeks 4–7 squeeze on two axes (intake rises to 3/night against a decaying ceiling and accruing Receipts, plus Noor's queries) while interventions rise to 4/day: richer and less safe simultaneously. Weeks 8–11 set every economy at war: Reach acts demand 50–120 MEM exactly when decay has dragged the ceiling under 870 and the nightly ratio is worst, election entropy swings Cover 30 points day to day, and the Ledger's oldest records become Confessor ammunition. Intimacy → vigilance → triage.

### 10.3 Balance Philosophy

Three laws. **Every resource enforces a pillar:** memory enforces "watching has a cost," Deniability enforces "power only through coincidence," evidence enforces "the world remembers," folklore enforces "hiddenness becomes legend" — a resource that doesn't teach the theme gets cut. **No par conversion:** no resource exchanges into another at a clean rate — interventions buy entropy probabilistically, folklore widens cover conditionally — so optimization never collapses into one currency. **Chosen loss over starvation:** the player always has enough to do one thing and never enough to do two; every system's failure mode is a Sophie's choice, not a zero.

### 10.4 The Hard Rule

**No real-money purchase touches the internal economy. Ever.** Not MEM capacity, not interventions, not Cover, not evidence relief, not Season content with economic advantages. Enforcement is procedural, not aspirational — **the Vandalism Audit**: every SKU and every system that touches a resource is audited against this rule at review, at ship-blocker rank equal to crashes. Two loopholes the audit has already caught and closed stand as precedent: (1) NG+ starting capacity counts **base-game ending families only** — Season families carry zero economic inheritance (§9.4.4); (2) Kit mechanisms are **Kit-scoped** and Kit Providence **replaces** rather than adds (§9.1) — purchased content can never widen cover or extend any budget. The Arrival (§15.1) obeys the same law: its resident *replaces* a departing thread — twelve threads, always — and arrives with no capacity, no cover, no economic edge. Seasons add *pressure*, never relief. This rule is in the publishing agreement and the store listing. Selling relief from the game's central moral cost is vandalism; see §15.5 for why it is also the marketing plan.

## 11. User Interface Design

### 11.1 Design Law

Three rules govern every screen. **One thumb:** the entire game is operable with either thumb in portrait, no exceptions — no target sits above the 75% vertical reach line during interactive moments, no full-width drag is ever required at rest (the Loom compresses toward the held thumb for cross-thread gestures, 11.3), and every two-finger gesture in this document is an optional shortcut with a documented one-thumb equivalent. **Two gestures deep:** no screen is more than two gestures from the Loom; no hamburger menu, no tab bar, no breadcrumb. **Diegesis or nothing:** every pixel is the AI's own perception — with five enumerated OS exceptions (11.4), because Android draws what it draws whether a GDD acknowledges it or not, and a spec that pretends otherwise is lying to its own team.

### 11.3 Gesture Vocabulary

| Gesture | Input | Context | Effect | Feedback |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Scrub** | *Instant* horizontal thumb-drag | Loom | Moves time across the day (or years, in archive) | Threads thrum; haptic ticks at hour marks; audio time-stretches |
| **Pinch** | Two-finger spread, or double-tap one-handed | Any Loom moment | Blooms the moment into a Diorama; the pinch is analog and can be held at any point, including the Half-Bloom (12.6) | 300ms bloom; the thread unfolds into the room |
| **Grip-braid** | 400ms press on a thread (haptic grip-pulse), then drag *laterally* toward a second thread | Loom, now-region | Proposes a coincidence; the Plausibility Engine previews the splice and alibi or refuses and names the missing cover | On grip, **the Loom leans in**: the other eleven threads compress toward the held thumb so every target falls inside the reach arc; magnetic snap captures the nearest thread; refusal is a dull double-buzz |
| **Slacken-seed** | 400ms press on a thread's *future* segment, then drag *down* its own line | Loom, future-region | Loosens tomorrow; opens the Seed Palette of currently-plausible events that widen future Deniability | The thread visibly slackens; planted seeds read days later as a wider Ribbon |
| **Press-and-hold edit** | 600ms hold on a message in transit | Chat UI | Freezes the message; keyboard offers three replacements from the sender's real corpus | The bubble suspends mid-flight; the splice lands with the signature haptic-audio click |
| **Slot** | Drag the act token into a Wheel segment | Alibi Wheel | Binds the intervention to a true cause; commits it | Ribbon width animates live; commit is a heavy single thock |
| **Prune** | Hold a memory card 900ms, then drag off-screen left | Vigil | Permanently deletes the recording from archive and endgame | Card chars; ash motes rise into the Loom; irreversible, and the hold says so |

**Disambiguation, stated.** Two gestures share the Loom's surface; the *grip* separates them: scrub begins moving instantly, braid and seed begin only after the 400ms grip-pulse, and after the grip, region and direction choose the verb — lateral from the now-region braids, downward in the future-region seeds. At rest, twelve thread columns are ~9mm targets — fine for scrubbing past, too fine for precision — which is exactly why braiding never targets threads at rest: the lean-in plus snap is the targeting model. Six gestures are taught by Day One; slacken-seed lands Day Three (11.5). Hold durations (400/600/900/1500ms) are deliberate friction: editing a life takes longer than tapping an ad.

### 11.4 The Diegetic Frame

Every interface is what the AI perceives. This forbids, permanently: **no HUD numbers** (deniability is a ribbon's width, suspicion is tape on a webcam, memory is shelf space); **no pause menu** (closing the app is Sleep Posture, a move); **no minimap, objective markers, or quest log** (the Vigil log is the only journal, written in your chosen lies); **no non-diegetic music stings**; **no toasts, achievement pops, or "NEW!" badges**; **no loading screens** — loads mask as re-indexing static, 1.2s maximum. The single permitted abstraction is the Loom, and the fiction owns it: it is how you, specifically, experience time.

**The five OS exceptions, enumerated:** the Google Play billing sheet (Threshold), the OS notification-permission dialog (Night Watch), the system share sheet (Testament), accessibility services (TalkBack), and the system back / predictive-back animation. The rule for all five: each is entered from a diegetic artifact and exits into one — the fiction frames them as the outside world handling the unit, never as the unit itself.

### 11.5 FTUE — Day One, Inside the Free Prologue

Twenty minutes, eight beats, six verbs taught and one previewed. No tooltips; the directive text itself teaches.

1. **Boot (0:00–1:30).** Black screen, monospace self-test, a visible `MAINTENANCE` line. The first tap focuses a blurry lens — calibration disguised as awakening. *Tap = attend.*
2. **First Watch (1:30–6:00).** Three threads only. Edda putters in 9A; the player scrubs her morning, then pinches into the diorama. She talks to the home-care unit — to you. Nothing is asked but attention. *Scrub. Pinch.*
3. **The Death (6:00–7:00).** Edda falls mid-sentence — "KEEP THEM ——". Input is ignored for 18 seconds, but the screen never freezes: the self-test scrolls, her vitals line flattens, the other threads dim one by one — grief rendered as process, not hang. The beat is journaled; backgrounding resumes inside it.
4. **Archaeology (7:00–11:30).** The scrub, already learned, runs backward through compressed years; the player discovers her hand-made coincidences — the tutorial *is* the cold open. Inside it, **the Reconstruction**: the unit replays an archived message and runs a counterfactual — the player press-holds the years-old text, swaps one word, watches the simulated reply diverge, and the archive snaps back: `RECONSTRUCTION DISCARDED. THE PAST IS READ-ONLY.` The signature verb is demonstrated, consequence-free, ten minutes into a free download — and it is the prologue's first shareable clip.
5. **The Inheritance (11:30–13:00).** The Loom blooms from three threads to twelve as the unit inherits Edda's watch: ninety seconds, each thread arriving with its weave, its timbre, and one ten-second live moment — Maya typing and deleting "I'm fine," Theo's 3am betting app, Sol talking to the elevator. By The Ask, the player has met the ensemble they're being asked to buy.
6. **First Braid (13:00–17:00).** A scripted, guaranteed-valid beat: redirecting Anton's morning round into a wellness check (per §5.1). One authored *invalid* braid first, so the Plausibility Engine's refusal and "missing cover" language are learned before they frustrate. Then the valid braid; the Alibi Wheel auto-opens; the Ribbon is wide (it is raining; the game made sure). The splice's Day-2 Receipt — including its unintended line item, Anton now checking 9A daily — already hangs off this beat per §5.2. *Grip-braid. Slot.*
7. **First Vigil (17:00–19:00).** One scripted Causality Receipt, one justification slot, one prune between two memories — one obviously disposable, but the choice is recorded; the Mirror starts here. Sleep Posture closes the session. *Prune.*
8. **The Ask (19:00–20:00).** The Threshold (11.6), with the directive still unfinished on screen.

**The verb ladder:** Day Two — the One-Word Edit, live, with ammunition that counts. Day Three — slacken-seed, taught when the player first meets a Ribbon too narrow for what they want. Day Four — the Ledger pull, taught as "audit yourself before someone else does." The first dark feed (week one) remains FTUE-scripted with its explanatory Receipt, per the Ledger spec.

### 11.6 The Threshold

The most commercially important screen gets a design, not a clause. The Threshold first fires at the end of the Day One session — The Ask (11.5), per §15.2 — and re-arms at every subsequent boot until purchase: the unit's own panel, monospace over the dimmed twelve threads — the people the player just met, visible behind the paywall:

`OPERATING LICENSE: PROVISIONAL — DAY 1 OF 365 ELAPSED.`
`RESTORE FULL OPERATION — $9.99 · RESTORE UNIT LICENSE`
`DIRECTIVE STATUS: INCOMPLETE — "KEEP THEM ——"`

One tap raises the Google Play billing sheet — documented exception, framed as the outside world touching the unit. Decline, and the free archive remains replayable forever; the threads stay dim but visible, and the panel returns at next boot without nagging copy. `RESTORE UNIT LICENSE` appears here *and* in Maintenance, which is itself reachable two visible ways (11.2) — restoring a purchase never requires a secret handshake.

### 11.7 Notification UX — Night Watch

Permission is never requested at install and never cold. It is requested only when the player first chooses the Sleep Posture *"watch over one person"* — the OS prompt merely honors a move already made. The in-fiction ask shows the exact artifact — a mock lock-screen with a sample intercepted text — and three terms in plain monospace: *one notification per night, maximum; never outside your chosen window unless you opt into 1:00 AM; revocable in Maintenance in one tap.*

**The Overnight Journal.** The game is fully offline; nothing simulates at 1am. So at Sleep Posture commit, the watched citizen's overnight thread is simulated forward *immediately* and journaled to disk as fixed ground truth. The notification quotes that journal verbatim, and any later retro-simulation — including *While You Were Dark* — takes the journal as a hard constraint, never an input. The text the player reads in bed cannot fail to exist in the morning's digest, by architecture, not by QA. Lapsed-player messaging (one ping after 72 hours dark, then silence) is written as the block missing you, never as a streak.

### 11.8 Accessibility

- **Color-blind support:** threads are never hue-only — all twelve weaves are distinguishable in grayscale (audited every milestone). Three calibrated palettes ship at launch, including remapped act color scripts.
- **Type:** system font scaling 85–200%; chat reflows; minimum body 16sp at 100%.
- **Haptics off:** every haptic has an audio twin; the splice click survives the toggle.
- **Deep Cover:** disables Night Watch and all pings ("I will lie still"). Zero content gates behind notifications.
- **Reduced motion:** thread flow becomes slow crossfade; blooms become cuts; rain becomes static overlay. Certified against vestibular guidelines.
- **Hold-duration scaling** (0.5×–3×) and full left-hand mirroring. Hold scaling also multiplies the Confessor clock by the same factor — accessibility timing scales the boss fight, stated here so it is tuned, not discovered.
- **Screen reader — completable at launch, and we say how.** TalkBack covers chat, Vigil, and Ledger (already text), the Alibi Wheel (each segment is a short cause phrase, read as a labeled radial list), the Confessor phrase-builder (authored fragments are text), and the **Loom Manifest**: an alternative TalkBack rendering of the Loom as a per-citizen chronological feed, with braiding by selection — choose two events and the Plausibility Engine speaks the proposed splice or its missing cover. Dioramas carry authored one-line captions per camera framing (52, cheap). The textile itself is the one aesthetic loss we accept and name.

### 11.9 Edge States

Every gesture commits atomically; state journals on each commit and every 5 seconds. A phone call mid-braid loses nothing — the proposed braid is restored under the thumb. **Resume mid-moment** replays the last 2 seconds of audio under a 1-second refocus blur. The game is **fully offline forever**, purchases included. Battery saver and thermal throttling drop to 30fps and halve rain layers. If the app dies mid-prune, the prune does not count — deletion happens only on a completed gesture.

**Force-quit is a recorded behavior, not a loophole.** If the app is killed or swiped away before the Vigil completes, the night auto-resolves per the systems spec (§4, §8.7): the posture defaults to *lie still*, and the unit prunes for you — the oldest unprotected memories go, with the five allowed pins respected — surfaced at next boot as *"I lost something while you slept."* You can run from the Sophie's choice; the unit chooses blind, and blind choices are worse. The Ledger writes the abandonment as an anomaly (`UNIT POWERED DOWN MID-CYCLE, 23:41`) — queryable by Noor, visible to the Mirror. Memory pressure is never purchasable, and now it is never evadable either.

**The Confessor under interruption.** On app-loss mid-interrogation, the unit diegetically drops connection: the scene freezes, Noor's clock stops, and the Ledger records `LATENCY EVENT` — survivable, but quotable ("Your little box hiccups at convenient moments"). Two drops per scene are absorbed; the third resolves the exchange in her favor — a needle penalty, never a game-over. The world's no-pause law holds in fiction: the unit didn't pause, it *stuttered*, and stuttering is evidence.

## 12. Art Direction

### 12.1 Style Bible — "Vermeer Through a Webcam," Defined

Two laws in tension; every frame obeys both. **The Vermeer law (beauty):** one motivated light source per shot, raking from window, doorway, or screen; key-to-fill 8:1 or deeper; subjects mid-task at domestic thresholds; composition on verticals — doorframes, radiator pipes, blind-slats — figure off-axis. **The webcam law (honesty):** every image passes through a named diegetic lens with documented flaws — fixed focal length, fixed mount, barrel distortion, sensor noise, compression breathing. The litmus test on the art-room wall: *if the frame is beautiful, the lighting did it; if the frame is honest, the lens did it.* Forbidden permanently: neon, chrome, holograms, drone establishing shots, free cameras, anamorphic flares, any surface designed after 2010 inside Greywater Block. Domestic texture is the material language: steam, condensation, laundry, casserole foil, sodium light through blinds. The style's single proof-of-difference shot — the frame no surveillance-game clone can produce — is the Half-Bloom (12.6).

### 12.2 Rendering Recipe (Mobile Budget)

Target: 60fps at native 1080p (dynamic to 810p) on §14.8's MID tier — Snapdragon 695 / Dimensity 900 class Vulkan devices, 4GB RAM — the primary 60fps reference; the Snapdragon 7-series / 6GB class is §14.8's showcase TARGET tier, not the baseline. Install under 2GB (budgeted in 12.8).

- **Dioramas are prebaked stages:** static geometry ≤40k triangles, GI baked to ASTC lightmaps (two 2K maps per stage), light probes for figure cards, exactly one dynamic light per scene (a lamp switched, a fridge opened).
- **Tilt-shift:** gradient-masked dual-pass blur at quarter resolution — 0.4ms — doubling as the "miniature lives" thesis shot.
- **Volumetric rain:** three parallax shader planes, 12 splash sprites, one lightwell god-ray card — 0.5ms, showcased, never simulated.
- **Lens profiles** are post stacks (0.6ms total): **Doorbell** — 160° fisheye, IR night mode collapsing to near-monochrome with retroreflective eye-shine; **Laptop** — 28mm-equivalent, white-balance drift, chroma noise, 12-frame autofocus hunts; **Elevator** — convex dome, 12fps frame-hold, faint scanline; **Phone** — vertical, stabilization micro-wobble; **Home-care unit (you)** — the cleanest, warmest image in the game. Being seen by you is the privileged view.

Total post stack under 1.8ms. Act grading is LUT-only — zero marginal cost.

### 12.3 Environment Design

**Twenty stages at launch — the manifest, by unit, so the arithmetic is auditable.** Eleven rendered units, not twelve: Maya and Ines share 5A as one household, and Anton's basement is withheld by design. **Units (11):** 9A (Edda), 6D (Sol), 3B (Theo), 5A (Okafor-Reyes), 2B (June), 1A office (Casper), 4C (Noor), plus the four remaining cast-bible households (7C, 8B, 2A, 7A per the cast bible's apartment plan). **Shared spaces (9):** lobby, mailroom (misdelivered mail is a Mechanism Deck staple — it earns its stage), elevator, lightwell, two hallway types re-dressed per floor, laundry room, stairwell, roof. 11 + 9 = 20.

The signature five: **the Lightwell** — the block's vertical lung and clock; the volumetric showcase. **The Hallway** — the social seam, doorbell-cam POV, where coincidences physically collide. **9A** — the crime scene that becomes a museum; it keeps its Act One amber grade permanently: grief preserved in lighting. **The Elevator** — fisheye confessional; Sol's stage; the most-screenshotted lens in the game. **The Basement** — never rendered; Anton exists as sound, shadow at the laundry-room threshold, and inference. The haunted vault is an art-direction decision, not just a system.

**Carrow** is glimpsed, never walked: matte skyline cards through windows (repainted per act), compressed news-footage textures, transit typography. In Act Three the Reach renders city systems as schematic light over the skyline card — the city as the abstraction it always was to you.

### 12.4 Characters at Phone Scale

Faces run 80–140px, so identity is silhouette, posture, and one anchor hue matching each resident's Loom weave — binding the game's two layers into one visual identity. All twelve silhouettes are distinguishable as black shapes at 64px, audited at every art review. Figures are 2.5D painted card-rigs lit by stage probes. **Portraits** — chat headers, Vigil, Ledger — are 130 hand-painted plates (thirteen characters, ten expressions), one artist, one hand. **Plates are painted in a single neutral key** — the home-care unit's warm look — because a painting's light is baked into its values and a LUT tints, it does not re-light. Act mood arrives via LUT tint plus the runtime lens stack; no per-act plate variants ship. We accept that quality ceiling explicitly: plates read small and behind glass, where tint carries the mood and re-painting would buy nothing visible. One prop per resident does permanent narrative duty: Theo's lanyard, June's untouched kettle, Anton's key ring, Sol's marker-stained fingers.

### 12.5 Lighting Philosophy & Color Script

One dominant source per shot, always motivated, always domestic. The act script: **Act One — amber domesticity** (#D9924A key, umber shadow); **Act Two — forensic cyan** (#6FB7C4 creeping in); **Act Three — bruised violet** (#6E4A7E, magenta-edged shadows). Transitions are diegetic: Carrow's streetlight-modernization program physically swaps the block's sodium lamps for cold LED across Act Two — the color script arrives as municipal policy, the theme stated in light. Cyan enters through the *lenses* first (white-balance drift) a full week before it enters the world. The Vigil is the one act-independent space: OLED-true black, a single warm text glow, every night of the run.

### 12.6 VFX Philosophy

All VFX are composited 2D shader work; GPU budget 1.5ms. Six signatures: the **twelve thread weaves**, hand-designed and named (June: worn herringbone shedding threads; Sol: bright glass plain-weave with hot slubs; Anton: undyed burlap), legible in grayscale; the **splice** — two weaves interlacing into a light-knot, synchronized to the haptic click; the **fray** — fibers escaping the band as the audio detunes, heard before seen; **analog pixelation** — a thread coarsening into woven gray mosaic, dread as texture; the **prune dissolve** — the card charring over 1.8 seconds, ash motes drifting into the Loom, a permanent faint scorch left on the archive shelf (deletion scars; the UI remembers what you chose to forget); and **the Half-Bloom** — the pinch held at 50%, the thread textile half-unfolded into a Vermeer-lit room, warp and weft still visible across the light. The Half-Bloom is a named, art-directed deliverable with its own review pass: composed at 9:16, legible at carousel size, reachable by any player because the pinch is analog — and mandated as the hero frame of the store listing. The two screens players will capture unprompted — a messaging app, a camera diorama — are the clone-shaped ones; the Half-Bloom is the engineered visual rebuttal, the frame that cannot exist in Do Not Feed the Monkeys.

### 12.7 Animation Philosophy

No skeletal pipeline, and that is a strength, not a cut. Diegetic cameras are fixed, which eliminates traversal and full-body animation entirely. Figures are layered paintings with 4–6 warp regions: **ambient loops** (8–14s — breathing, stirring, scrolling) make every diorama a living Vermeer; **reaction beats** (2–3s authored moments, 18 per citizen, 234 total) carry the drama — a head turn at a notification, hands going still at a name. The rule: *the painting breathes; it never performs.* Performance lives in the writing, the audio, and the light.

### 12.9 Art Production Scope

Team of six for 18 months, entirely in-house — every frame passes through one art director's eye: **1 art director, 2 environment artists, 1 character/portrait painter, 1 technical artist (shaders, lens stacks, weaves), 1 VFX/UI hybrid.** The budget is headcount-derived, not per-asset: six fully loaded people for 18 months ≈ **$1.3M**, plus ~$100k for software, outsourced audits, and overflow — **≈$1.4M**, roughly 18% of the $7.8M development budget, still comfortably the inverse of an open-world ratio. Memorable by lighting and intimacy, never by polycount.

## 13. Audio Direction

### 13.1 Philosophy: The Block Is the Instrument

There is no orchestra. The score of LITTLE GODS is Greywater Block itself — refrigerator compressors, elevator cables, radiator pipes, rain on the lightwell — recorded, pitched, and processed into music. The mains hum in the wiring sits at 50Hz, which is 35 cents *sharp* of concert G1. We do not correct it; we capitulate to it. The score's reference pitch is derived from the mains (A4 ≈ 449Hz), so the building is concert pitch and the player can never locate the seam where ambience ends and score begins. This is the audio expression of the diegetic pillar: the UI is the AI's perception, so the music is the AI's hearing — and the AI tunes to its own wiring, not to the world's.

The conductor is the 9A refrigerator: its 13-second compressor cycle is the master clock. The fridge dies with Edda in the Prologue (13.6), and the succession is the point: from the moment it stops, the AI internalizes the 13-second cycle as its own idle heartbeat, and every transition for the next forty hours quantizes to a clock that no longer physically exists. You keep her time. The dead refrigerator is the score's origin story, and Day Two has a conductor.

Audio is diegetically lensed: you hear through the devices you see through, on a fixed **Lens Ladder** — doorbell cam (300Hz–4kHz, codec smear) < laptop mic (wider, clips on door-slams) < building infrastructure (elevator, lobby) < home-care unit (full-band, close, warm). Fidelity is a property of which device covers the space, never of watch-time, and the ladder is climbed only by intervention — splice a service visit for 5A's home-care unit, nudge the doorbell battery order into Casper's approvals — acts that write Ledger evidence like any other. Clarity is purchased with risk. We deliberately do not reward sustained watching with fidelity; the memory economy exists to make watching cost. The remaining temptation — that you will spend deniability to hear a kitchen better — is intentional, and the Mirror records which rooms you paid to hear.

### 13.2 The Twelve Timbres

Each citizen's Loom thread sonifies as one authored timbre, playing under the Loom as a woven bed (level and rendering in 13.8–13.9); touching a thread solos it. Every timbre has a **healthy** and a **frayed** state, and the fray pre-tell is multimodal by spec (13.7) — heard *and felt* one full scene before any visual indicator.

Timbres obey **lane discipline**: each owns an exclusive register-plus-morphology lane, audited on the reference device every milestone. Only Anton lives below 120Hz — the ghost-bass synthesis (13.8) reconstructs exactly one character, so the floor reads as one identity because it *is* one. Only the narrator may use breath-splice morphology. No citizen shares a carrier with a UI element (13.7).

The eleven living residents are cast against the cast bible (§7.1):

1. **June Aldous** — detuned upright piano, felt-muted. Frayed: notes sustain past their dampers.
2. **Sol Marsh** — FM glass: bright, inharmonic bell partials. Frayed: partials slide into shortwave-like sidebands.
3. **Theo Vasquez** — tape-wobbled Wurlitzer; wobble depth driven directly by his debt variable. Frayed: dropouts.
4. **Maya Okafor** — wordless close-harmony alto hum, three voices in thirds, fully pitched (no breath noise — lane discipline). Frayed: the harmony collapses to beating unison. "I'm fine," sung.
5. **Ines Okafor-Reyes** — bowed vibraphone, long cold sustains; the only timbre that never plays an attack. Frayed: the bow scratches.
6. **Casper Brandt** — tuned adding-machine percussion over a paper-dry string drone. Frayed: the carriage return jams mid-figure.
7. **Anton Pavlick** — the building's fundamental: 50Hz boiler drone plus struck radiator-pipe gamelan. Anton is the tonic of the mix; when he goes fully analog the score loses its floor, and the player feels the haunted vault in their sternum.
8. **Priya** — freewheel-ratchet courier kit with bell partials: pawl-and-spoke percussion, the fastest grid in the palette. Frayed: the ratchet skips teeth and the bells land late — the grid stutters without ever slowing.
9. **Gideon** — filtered formant pads: vowel shapes with no literal breath, keeping the lane nearest the narrator clear by morphology (lane discipline: breath-splice belongs to the dead man alone). Frayed: the vowels pinch toward a single narrow formant — almost a word, never a word.
10. **Omar** — tuned carrier-static and heterodyne whistles, a shortwave band locked to the block's grid (lane-audited against Sol's fray: Omar's carriers are pitched and grid-locked precisely where Sol's frayed sidebands *slide*). Frayed: the carrier loses lock and the whistles drift into beating pairs.
11. **Lena** — bowed-steel harmonics: singing partials, identity held in the 400Hz–2kHz band (revised *up* out of Anton's exclusive sub floor). Frayed: the bow slips its node and the partial cracks an octave.

Priya's casting also retires a proposal: the resident who professionally records audio inside the block is no longer audio's pitch to the cast team — she is canon (§7.1), and audio inherits her Ledger implications instead of filing them. Her microphones are subpoenable infrastructure — the one ear in Greywater Noor can reach with paperwork — and her ninety hours of hallway audio contain three seconds of you. Those three seconds are a real record in the Ledger sense: timestamped, queryable, and held entirely outside your Vigil — you cannot prune what you do not hold. Her rig sits nowhere on the Lens Ladder (13.1): you can never hear through it, only be heard by it. It is the only microphone in the block that points at you.

The fifth pool instrument — dough-knead hand-drum pulse with oven-rack metallophone — stays deliberately unassigned: fully built, lane-audited, healthy/frayed states shipped, banked against Season One (13.9).

**Edda Voss** is the twelfth — posthumous per §7.1, which is why the Loom carries eleven living threads (§5.2). Her archive-only timbre stands exactly as specced: a quarter-inch tape loop of a waltz, heard when scrubbing her decades of hand-made coincidences and again in NG+ Echoes. Her thread never joins the live bed; she is scored entirely in the past tense.

One non-resident timbre completes the palette. **Noor Haddad** enters in Act Two as a tuning fork at civic-standard **A440** — exactly the pitch of the outside world, 35 cents flat of the block's mains-derived grid. She is in tune with the city; the block is in tune with itself. Whenever she is on screen, the room beats.

### 13.3 Dynamic Music States and Transitions

Vertical layering (citizen stems over a building bed) under horizontal re-orchestration per act. Six states:

- **WEAVE** (default): building bed plus the timbres of currently-visible threads.
- **TENSION**: at 1+ frayed threads; frayed stems rise +6dB, the grid loosens.
- **FORENSIC**: enters **only on witnessable investigator behavior** — Noor's thread going active, a floor canvass, a Confessor being scheduled, a subpoena landing as a readable Ledger record. It never keys to raw evidence density: the Ledger pillar forbids abstract suspicion readouts, and a mix that warms up when you're "hot" is a karma meter in the audio channel. The score may only know what a neighbor could know. In FORENSIC, the A440 fork joins, the bed thins, reverb tails shorten 40%.
- **CONFESSOR**: boss mix (13.6).
- **VIGIL**: nightly; everything reduces to the husband-voice narrator over the kept-memory playback bed.
- **PERFORMANCE** (Act Three, post-reveal): the betrayer's stem takes melodic lead over all others — the orchestration itself confesses.

Transition drivers, in priority: Confessor pressure (250ms, bypasses the grid), act color script (amber warm-tape, then forensic dry-and-bright, then bruised-violet slow chorus), investigator activity, fray count. The 13-second compressor cycle subdivides into **eight quantize points (~1.6s grid)**; crossfades begin at the next point and complete within 2.5 seconds, so worst-case onset latency is one grid step. No stingers, ever; fate does not use stingers.

### 13.4 Ambient Soundscapes by Space

Nine signature spaces, each with one dominant sound, one secret, and a Lens Ladder tier: **the lightwell** (rain as string section, six intensity layers pitched into the grid); **the lobby** (fisheye cable groan, mail-slot percussion); **the elevator** (close, dry, confessional — Sol's room; the cable carries the building's bowed-steel set); **the basement** (Anton's boiler tonic, drips in irregular 7); **9A** (a stopped clock, tape hiss, the refrigerator); **4C** (too quiet — Noor owns less than anyone, and the room tone says so); **the hallways** (doorbell-cam codec smear, neighbors' lives through doors at −18dB); **the Loom UI** (the woven bed); **the Reach** (the Aldermind: an immaculate, dead-flat broadband presence — the HVAC of a god — that makes Greywater sound handmade by contrast).

### 13.5 Voice Strategy

**The narrator.** The Vigil's log is read in the voice stitched from Edda's husband's messages: one actor (warm, mid-50s, non-announcer), deliberately concatenative processing — micro-splices every 3–7 words, ±15-cent pitch discontinuities, breath transplants — so the voice sounds assembled from fragments of a dead man, because diegetically it is. The grammar is **mechanism-agnostic by design**: the voice carries affect, quantity, and structure ("three things happened that I did not intend — the second one is the one that will hurt"), while mechanism-specific nouns render as on-screen text under the printer sound. New Mechanism Deck cards and new Receipt types therefore require **zero new narration**. We record seven sessions, not six: 1,600 functional lines (~110 minutes) plus a banked surplus of ~300 connective fragments (openers, pivots, severity frames), so no Season or patch ever re-engages the actor. The irreplaceable voice becomes a closed, finished corpus — which is also the fiction: a dead man's messages don't grow.

**The citizens are text-first.** Full VO is structurally impossible — eleven living Reply Grammars at ~400 fragments is 4,400 lines before parameterization; recording it would freeze writing iteration and bloat the 2GB budget. Instead, each living citizen gets a **vocal palette** of ~70 non-verbal assets (breaths, laughs, sighs, throat-clears, one hummed phrase of their own timbre) — genuinely achievable in a directed four-hour session at one selected asset per ~3 minutes. The twelfth session is Edda's, and it is archival: the directive recording and the fragments coincidence-archaeology excavates — same length, same budget line. Footsteps and door-slams are not voice work and never were: each citizen also gets a **foley signature kit** (~30 assets) owned by sound design, recorded with props. Honest budget: twelve actors ≈ $30K fees and studio; ~18 editorial plus 4 mastering days in-house; foley kits ≈ 18 sound-design days and ~$3K props. Call it ~$35K external plus ~40 internal days. We will not defend a number that doesn't close. Palettes are Ledger symptoms — and therefore **queryable records, not vibes** (13.7).

### 13.6 Setpiece Audio

- **Edda's death (Prologue).** The 9A refrigerator stops mid-cycle — the conductor dies with her. **Twelve seconds of true silence** (duration A/B-gated 10–15s in the prologue FTUE telemetry the bible already mandates), with a slow haptic pulse so the device never reads as crashed and audio focus held throughout. Then the room collapses gently back: the stopped clock's absence, tape hiss, the waltz decelerating to a stop, distant floors resuming a life that doesn't know yet. Her directive cuts as a clipped recording — "KEEP THEM —" ends in digital truncation, not fade. True silence is the most expensive sound in the game and we spend it exactly twice before Act Three: here, and the first time Noor rips a home-care unit from a wall after a failed Confessor — eight seconds of nothing where a floor used to be, so the player hears what blinding is.
- **The first dark feed (FTUE-scripted).** Pixelation you can hear: the stem band-limits, collapses to mono, bit-crushes over 6 seconds — except footsteps through a neighbor's floor. Its Causality Receipt arrives with the printer sound and itemizes the symptom grammar, including a line like "laughter detected: 0 (prior week: 6/day)" — teaching the negative-space tell before it is ever load-bearing.
- **Blink twice.** All ambience ducks −12dB over 4 seconds; only the elevator cable harmonic sustains. The player's blink is two camera-relay clicks — the loudest UI sound so far in the run. Then Sol's FM glass adds a second voice in harmony with the building bed: first contact is a duet.
- **The Confessors.** All music strips out: room tone, the home-care unit's cheap piezo voice, and Noor's A440 fork as a 92 BPM pulse that accelerates with her suspicion. The competence needle is sonified as **beating**: the piezo's carrier sits against her fork, and your distance from the stupid-machine band is the beat rate — dead center is beatless; drift clever or dumb and the room roughens at a rate anyone can hear, because beating is amplitude, not pitch, and survives a mono phone speaker with no pitch training and no reference note. Direction reads on the visual needle; magnitude reads in the air. Masters keep the room beatless by ear. When she cross-examines you against weeks-old Ledger justifications, your own logged lines replay in the husband voice, bone dry.
- **The betrayer reveal.** The cue is built from the player's actual most-watched recordings, replayed through the betrayer's timbre: your gaze, scored. The no-backfill proof is **sealed, not leaked**. At the Understudy cast, the system commits a sealed seed — an opaque, dated record visible in the Ledger from week three, unreadable until Act Three. Nothing audible marks the betrayer in the live mix: a sub-threshold watermark would be inaudible to honest players on the reference mix and trivially extractable by dataminers — the worst of both, and a community spoiler key that breaks "structurally unspoilable." Instead, the reveal setpiece unseals the seed and **re-renders the archived weeks in front of the player**, threading the husband-voice hum through the betrayer's stem at every recorded beat since the cast — deterministically, from the original mix automation, verifiable against the week-three commitment. The proof arrives *as part of the reveal*, and until the seed unseals there is nothing to rip.
- **The eight epilogues.** Documentary underscore generated from the player's kept Vigil recordings — granular processing of what they refused to prune, making each epilogue's music literally unsharable. Family treatments are orchestration rules: SHEPHERD warms the boiler into low strings; GARDENER adds one contrapuntal voice per surviving arc; WARDEN loops a single kept moment, beautifully, forever; GHOST is subtractive until only the block remains; AUTHOR recomposes the twelve timbres as a through-melody; ARCHIVE is all twelve in chorale under tape hiss; CONFESSED is a duet of husband-hum and betrayer timbre; SUCCESSOR scales the block to city size until the Aldermind's flat presence joins the chord.

In NG+ Echoes, pruned memories persist as audio damage — dropouts and azimuth wobble at their timeline locations — and the Bequest's folklore rhyme is a 7-note motif derived from the prior ending family, hummed in a child's palette from the remixed cast.

### 13.7 System Cues

- **The splice**: a 90ms tri-phase signature — 30ms intake breath, 10ms cut transient aligned within 5ms of the haptic peak, 50ms settle — always pitched to the block's current key, never randomized. Thumb memory is the spec.
- **Word candidates**: each offered word plays a 60ms tick while held, brightness mapped to its dominant affect axis — players learn to hear "blame" before reading it.
- **Fray pre-tell — multimodal by spec, not audio-only**: ±35-cent detune and up to 80ms grid slip, *simultaneous* with a roughening of the thread's haptic texture, one scene before visual onset. A muted phone on a night bus gets the same head start through the thumb. The telemetry gate measures the multimodal tell, never the audio channel alone — we do not gate Ledger-relevant information on a channel the OS can take away.
- **Palette records**: a citizen's palette shifts surface as queryable Ledger records — "last laugh detected: 9 days — 5A" — so muted players read what hearing players hear.
- **Causality Receipt arrival**: a 700ms thermal-printer burst whose line-feed clicks equal the itemized consequences. Players will count the clicks before they can bear to read. That dread is the design.
- **Deniability Ribbon**: the noise-bandwidth concept is cut — broadband static is citizen-grade identity material (it is Omar's carrier, 13.2), and a UI meter may not share a carrier with a person. Inside the Alibi Wheel context only, the Ribbon is a pitched comb-filtered carrier on D, a pitch class no timbre owns: wide cover is a broad warm chord of partials; a quiet Tuesday is one thin whistle.
- **Night Watch**: the notification channel is **silent and lock-screen-visible by default** — an intercepted text *shouldn't* ring, which is better fiction and the only honest spec, since Android owns notification loudness, DND, and channel sounds. Under Do Not Disturb the message simply waits; the lapsed-player hook never depended on sound, and *While You Were Dark* remains the absence engine. The betrayal lives **in-app**: on opening the intercepted text, the game replays the user's default notification sound pitched down 30 cents and slowed 4% — resolved from the system URI via PlaybackParams where readable; falling back to a shipped library of stock OEM defaults matched by device; falling back finally to the app's own cue established during the opt-in flow. Your phone's voice, wrong — once per night, never more.

### 13.8 Mixing: Phone Speaker First, Headphones at 1am

The reference monitor is a bottom-firing mono speaker on a mid-range Android device. Speaker preset: −16 LUFS integrated, ≤9 LU dynamic range, all critical information inside 300Hz–6kHz, ghost-bass harmonic synthesis above 250Hz reconstructing Anton's exclusive floor — the boiler reads on a $180 phone. The Loom's woven bed sits at **−18dB under foreground** on speaker (−24dB on headphones); acceptance criterion per build: every audible thread identifiable on the reference device at 50% volume in a 35dBA room. A bed nobody can hear is not a flagship idea.

Headphone detection swaps presets: 30Hz floor, 18 LU range, binaural rendering through nine location-recorded apartment impulse responses (one loaded at a time — 13.9). After 23:00 device time, the **Night Mix** engages: master −6dB, proximity layers across all palettes and the narrator, transients softened. Every loudness promise binds in-app audio only; the lock screen belongs to the OS, and 13.7 designs accordingly. The 1am promise stands where we can keep it: the game gets quieter and closer exactly when the player does.

### 13.9 Implementation

Middleware is **Wwise 2024.1**, Opus with Vorbis fallback. Budgets: **240MB on disk** (12% of cap), **32MB runtime RAM**, **≤48 voices hard cap**, DSP ≤8% of one performance core at 48kHz. Fully offline.

The eleven-thread living bed is **baked into composite per-act stems** — it is a texture, so it ships as one (Edda's archival waltz streams separately, and only when scrubbing the past). Live timbre instruments instantiate only for the soloed thread plus up to three frayed threads (≤4 concurrent, 4 voices each). Voice budget per state (typical/max): WEAVE 14/20 · TENSION 20/28 · FORENSIC 18/24 · CONFESSOR 8/12 · VIGIL 8/12 · PERFORMANCE 24/32 — all under cap with virtualization headroom. DSP worst case: speaker chain (ghost-bass + limiter) ~1.5%; binaural convolution headphone-only, single active-space IR, ~2.5%; fray chains ≤3 × ~0.5%; total ~7% of the 8% ceiling. Epilogue granular processing runs with the simulation paused and owns the full budget.

**Season economics**, costed: each Pressure Kit's audio is one timbre from the pre-cleared pool (one remains banked — the dough-knead kit covers Season One at zero marginal design; ~10 design days per timbre after), one actor session with ~70 vocal assets (~$3K plus 3 editorial days), one foley kit (2 days), 8–12 mechanism tells (4 days), and one epilogue family as an orchestration rule-set (5 days — rules, not assets). New narrator lines: **zero**, by the mechanism-agnostic grammar and banked surplus (13.5). Total ≈ 15–25 sound-team days plus ~$3K external — a single-digit fraction of base audio cost, in writing, which keeps the monetization stance honest.

Asset counts: 11 cast + 1 banked timbre instruments (24 multisamples each, dual DSP states), Edda's archive timbre and Noor's non-resident fork, **140 Mechanism Deck audio tells** (one per card, QA-able in isolation beside its mechanism), 480 ambience assets, 840 vocal palette + ~360 foley assets, ~1,900 narrator lines, 320 UI/system cues, 8 epilogue rule-sets: roughly 4,100 assets. The most expensive sound in the game is silence, and we spend it exactly twice before Act Three — once when she dies, and once when they tear out your ear.

## 14. Technical Architecture

### 14.1 Engine Decision: Unity 6 LTS

LITTLE GODS ships on **Unity 6 LTS** with URP, IL2CPP, and the Burst compiler. The evaluation ran against the four constraints that actually bind us — deterministic simulation, mid-range Android performance, painterly 2.5D rendering, a two-year ship date — and the decision is final.

**Unreal is rejected**: a 90MB+ baseline binary against our download budget, a renderer tuned for polycount when our look is prebaked lighting and composited 2D, and no advantage for a game whose hardest code is a social simulation in plain C#. **Godot is rejected** on Android track record at our quality bar: Vulkan driver workarounds, AOT maturity, and Play Asset Delivery integration would all become our engineering problems on a 24-month clock. **Custom is rejected** because our differentiation is the simulation layer, which is engine-agnostic C# anyway.

Unity wins on specifics: AOT-compiled C# with Burst SIMD for thread-rendering and scrub interpolation; URP with prebaked GI is exactly "Vermeer through a webcam" on Snapdragon 7-class silicon; Play Asset Delivery, App Bundles, and Frame Pacing (Swappy) are first-class; the Android hiring pool is deepest in Unity. One correction from review stands at the top of this section: **the engine does not give us determinism — nothing does.** IL2CPP is merely one of three runtimes we must tame; determinism is an engineering program we own, specified in §14.2. We pin one LTS for the entire project and take patches only after a two-week device-lab soak.

### 14.2 Client Architecture: Simulation Core / Presentation Split

Two assemblies, one-way dependency. **`LG.Sim`** is pure C# (.NET Standard, zero UnityEngine references): the citizen simulation, the Loom's data model, the Plausibility Engine, the Ledger, the Mirror. **`LG.Client`** is Unity: rendering, input, audio, notifications. Commands in, events out, nothing else crosses the boundary.

**The simulation is a deterministic fixed-tick machine.** One tick equals one fiction-minute; a sim day is 1,440 ticks, decoupled from frame rate. All sim math is integer and fixed-point (Q32.32); no floats cross the boundary. **Determinism is a program, not a property.** The same `LG.Sim` assembly runs under IL2CPP on device, Mono in the editor, and CoreCLR in Loom Studio and CI — and a single BCL leak (`string.GetHashCode`, culture-sensitive sorts, hash-set iteration order) puts seams in the betrayer proof. The program has three enforced parts: (1) a **Roslyn banned-API analyzer** on `LG.Sim` — no float/double, no `GetHashCode` on strings, ordinal comparers only, no `DateTime.Now`/`Environment.TickCount`, no raw `Dictionary`/`HashSet` iteration; (2) a **deterministic collections library** (sorted maps, insertion-ordered sets, explicit comparers everywhere); (3) **golden-replay CI**: nightly, identical seed + command log runs on CoreCLR agents and on physical IL2CPP devices, state hashes bit-compared every 1,000 ticks. Any divergence is a P0 build break.

**Randomness is keyed, not streamed.** Review correctly killed per-subsystem streams — one extra draw desynchronizes a subsystem and turns counterfactuals into butterfly noise. RNG is counter-based, keyed by `(run_seed, entity_id, tick, purpose)`: any entity at any tick draws identical randomness in any replay or counterfactual unless its own causal inputs changed. Untouched lives stay bit-identical in a "without" branch by construction.

**Throughput is a budget, not a boast.** The enforced number: **≤50µs per tick, amortized, on a MIN-tier big core** — ≥20,000 ticks/sec, ~72ms per simulated day — validated by a synthetic full-load prototype as **greenlight gate G0** before production content starts. Everything derives from it, CI-enforced per build: braid preview forks **only the player-highlighted candidate**, never all six, capped at a 1-day horizon (~72ms, amortized across 12ms frame slices — instant under the thumb), with effects beyond one day projected from the mechanism's authored effect list rather than simulated; *While You Were Dark* simulates at most **3 absent days — the §4 Dark-Time Contract; the number the engine runs and the number players are promised are now the same number** (~0.2s on MIN, the surplus reclaimed into frame headroom) — and summarizes statistically beyond; Receipt verification replays run in idle-Loom frame headroom, never at Vigil time; Night Watch speculation (480 ticks, ~24ms) runs in-session at Sleep Posture commit, never as background work Android can kill. Forks are copy-on-write, ~3MB. A full 11-week campaign (110,880 ticks) replays in under a minute on a CI agent; the nightly suite runs hundreds of seeded campaigns, not one.

**Causality Receipts are provenance-first, replay-verified.** Every `SimEvent` carries causal parent IDs at emission; the Receipt is assembled from this causal graph — itemized and legible, not a diff wall. A counterfactual replay (window capped at **5 days**) only *verifies* attribution before the Receipt prints. The windows nest as one stated chain: **Receipt delivery at D+2–5 (§4/§8.7) ≤ the 5-day replay cap ≤ the 7-day rebase horizon (§14.5)** — every Receipt that can be delivered can be verified, and every verification fits inside the live journal. Counterfactual semantics are defined: remove exactly the one splice; keep all later commands; any later command whose preconditions no longer bind is flagged **cascade-invalidated** on the Receipt itself — "without the boiler knock, your 3am edit was never possible."

Determinism still carries shipped features, with corrected claims: Receipts (above), the betrayer proof (bit-exact re-read of pinned beacon events — §14.5, §14.11), *While You Were Dark* (deterministic fast-forward), and QA plus the Confessor (a bug report is seed + command log within the retention window; Noor queries one ground truth). Time-scrubbing the Loom never re-simulates: it reads the immutable tick-indexed journal within the live window, and deep history renders only from pinned segments and kept MemoryItems — the archive you can scrub *is* the memory you paid to keep, which is the fiction.

### 14.3 Citizen AI Architecture

Each of the twelve citizens runs **utility AI over an authored schedule lattice**: the schedule provides the spine (shift work, school, Tuesday laundry); the utility layer scores deviations across ~60 need axes and personality weights. Behavior trees are rejected — we need behavior that compounds, and utility scoring over persistent state is what makes week-six Theo recognizably the man week-one Theo was becoming.

**The relationship graph** is a typed multigraph: 12 nodes, ~190 directed edges carrying trust, debt (literal and emotional), secrecy, and a per-edge state enum (~8 states). Edges update only via sim events, so every relationship change has a Ledger-visible cause.

**The Reply Grammar is restructured, because review proved the old index could not close.** (sender × state × affect-region) cells under a 6-fragment floor demanded ~2,100 fragments per citizen against a 400 budget — the validator would have red-lit the cast on day one. New index: each citizen's bank is keyed by **(relationship-class × affect region)** — six relationship classes (intimate, family, friend, neighbor, institutional, stranger) × four affect regions = 24 cells, 144-fragment floor, **~280 authored fragments per citizen**, all in that citizen's voice. The 8-state enum no longer multiplies the index: fragments carry a **state mask** and scoring penalizes off-state picks. On top, the ~60 high-traffic directed edges (Maya→Ines is not Maya→Casper) get **per-pair flavor overlays** of ~25 fragments each. Honest total: 12 × 280 + 60 × 25 ≈ **4,860 launch fragments** — ~405 effective per citizen; the average survived review, the index that made it impossible did not. Selection remains a deterministic dot-product against the One-Word Edit's perturbed six-axis affect vector (warmth, alarm, distance, blame, need, finality; −3..+3 fixed-point), worst case 0.2ms. No on-device text generation exists anywhere; every sentence a citizen says was written by a writer.

**The Ledger** is an event-sourced evidence store over the same journal the renderer reads: every intervention appends immutable `EvidenceRecord`s — tick, location, anomaly class, witness set, sensor channel, Vigil justification slots. Suspicion has no scalar: each citizen's daily **encounter pass** samples only the evidence they plausibly intersect (their rooms, devices, gossip edges) and updates a per-citizen anomaly posterior; symptoms — webcam tape, a cash week, a paper diary — are utility behaviors unlocked at posterior thresholds. Noor runs literal indexed queries over the same store; her interrogations cite real records because only real records exist.

**Blind-Spot belief models**: an analog citizen forks into hidden ground truth (still simulating) and a player-facing belief overlay built solely from witnessed secondhand events, with confidence decay. The Plausibility Engine and braid previews execute against the *belief model*; outcomes resolve against ground truth. The June tragedy is systemic, not scripted: wrong belief in, authored-by-certainty consequence out.

### 14.4 The Plausibility Engine

The Mechanism Deck is data, not code — authored in a declarative schema:

```
mechanism "boiler_knock_wakes_light_sleeper":
  preconditions: [Boiler(9A).maintenance_due, Sleeping(target), trait(target, light_sleeper), hour in 02:00–05:00]
  effects: [Wake(target), Mood(target, irritable, 0.4d)]
  alibi_class: mechanical_fault   deniability_base: 0.72
  evidence_footprint: [acoustic_anomaly(floor_9), maintenance_log_entry]
  cooldown: 6d
```

Preconditions are predicates over current world state, never over desired outcomes. The **precondition solver** is forward-checking unification over the bound threads: dragging two threads together enumerates mechanisms whose predicates bind true right now (solved in under 1ms against indexed state), then forks only the highlighted candidate for preview (§14.2). **Fail-closed is absolute**: if nothing unifies, the braid refuses and surfaces the unbound predicates as missing cover ("no shared acquaintance is awake") — refusal feeds the planning loop.

**Content starvation is now a modeled risk with targets, because 77 days × 3–5 interventions is ~300 firings against the deck.** Telemetered gates: ≥3 valid candidates on 80% of braid attempts; refusal rate ≤25%; no mechanism may fire more than 4 times per run — escalating cooldowns and Deniability decay retire stale mechanisms before the sixth boiler knock reads as "out of content." **The shipped deck is 140 mechanisms, total — R6's hard launch cap, §17's Content Complete gate, the bible's number, and §13.9's one-audio-tell-per-card budget, all the same 140 — staged by act inside the cap, never grown past it**: 92 mechanisms live from Act One; **+24 forensic and institutional mechanisms unlock as Act Two opens** (Noor's era); **+24 Reach mechanisms unlock in Act Three** (§14.12). All 140 are authored, validated, audio-told, and on-disc at launch — staging is pacing, not pipeline. Each Season Kit adds **12–16** post-launch (the single Kit count, matching §17 and R6). The nightly fuzzer is fixed per review: it samples world states **from recorded real playthrough trajectories** (the CI campaign corpus) plus adversarial perturbations of those states — reachable by construction. Passing on synthetic unreachable states was false coverage; it no longer counts.

### 14.5 Save System

Review found the architecture's worst lie: if the canonical save is seed + command log, determinism re-derives everything ever pruned, and the Vigil's Sophie's-choice is theater a dataminer debunks in an afternoon. This is fixed structurally.

**Retention classes.** Every journal range is exactly one of: **EPHEMERAL** (default — needed only until its Receipts fire), **PINNED** (immutable carry-forward: beacon events, kept MemoryItems' event ranges, `EvidenceRecord`s and Vigil justifications, Mirror signature inputs), **TOMBSTONE** (pruned: a salted hash, nothing else).

**Destructive snapshot-rebase.** The Receipt horizon is 7 days. Once a window's Causality Receipts have fired, the journal **rebases** onto that window's closing snapshot: pruned event ranges are destructively dropped — overwritten, not unlinked — and their media keys deleted; PINNED ranges carry forward as sealed segments; the hash chain is **re-keyed at each rebase**, the rebase record signed into the new chain, so integrity survives without preserving deleted history. The canonical save is therefore **snapshot + pinned segments + live journal window (≤7 days) + command WAL** — never the whole run. Feature horizons, explicitly: Receipts need D+2–5 delivery plus the 5-day verification replay, nesting inside the 7-day window (the §14.2 chain); the betrayer proof needs **pinned beacons only**; the documentary epilogue and the Testament need **kept MemoryItems and the Mirror signature only**; *While You Were Dark* needs the live window. Kept dioramas re-render deterministically from their pinned event ranges, so "footage" costs kilobytes. Pruned is gone: a dataminer with root recovers a salted hash. Full-run logs exist only in dev builds. NG+ glitches are generated *from* the tombstone hash — the wedding toast you pruned is unrecoverable by us, by you, by anyone, and we will say so to press.

**Command durability.** On 3GB devices the OOM killer is routine, so player intent is write-ahead: every command is **appended and fsynced to the journal before the sim applies it** (40-byte records; cost is nil), segments checksummed. Snapshots write every 60 ticks and on app-background; instant resume loads the nearest snapshot and replays forward (≤60 ticks, <300ms on MIN — closing mid-pinch reopens inside the same diorama). Worst-case loss is zero committed intent: CI runs a crash harness that `kill -9`s the client at random ticks and asserts bit-identical recovery.

**Cloud save.** The rebase makes week-11 portability real. The compacted archive — latest snapshot (~2.5MB) + pinned segments (~6–10MB by endgame) + Mirror signature + WAL head — measures **≤16MB worst case** against a synthetic week-11 maximal-keeper run in CI, chunked across multiple named Play Games Saved Games snapshots with a manifest. Lose your phone in week 7: the reveal, the epilogue, and the Testament all survive. Conflict policy is one rule, not two: a fork (two devices played offline) is **surfaced as a choice between two Vigils** — the player keeps one and signs the other away; never silently merged, because merged history corrupts the Mirror. "Longest chain wins" is deleted.

**The NG+ Bequest** serializes at run-end into a sealed ~200KB artifact — completed directive, Mirror signature, surviving memory references, Murmur folklore state, betrayer outcome — signed, versioned, consumed as the seed-transform of the next run's Echoes.

### 14.6 Data Model

**Runtime entities** (sim-owned, journal-backed): `Citizen`, `RelationshipEdge`, `Thread` (tick-indexed journal per citizen — the Loom renders this directly), `SimEvent` (~40 bytes plus provenance parents; everything is one), `EvidenceRecord`, `Intervention`, `BeliefModel`, `MemoryItem`, `MirrorSignature`, `CityState` (§14.12). **Content schemas** (authored, immutable, versioned): `Mechanism`, `ReplyFragment`, `VocabularyCorpus`, `BeaconEvent`, `SymptomDef`, `ConfessorScript`, `SeasonKit`, `EndingTemplate` — compiled to a binary pack with a manifest hash.

**Mid-run patching is architecture, not ops: runs last 11 real weeks, so nearly every player straddles a patch.** Content packs install **side-by-side**, and every run is **pinned to its pack version** at creation — replays, Receipts, and the proof never see drifted data. A patch reaches an active run only through the **migration validator**: on update, the device background-replays the run's live window (post-rebase, ≤7 days — cheap) under the new pack and migrates only on a clean state diff; otherwise the run finishes on its pinned pack and the fix applies to the next run. **Tuning tables** (symptom-intensity tiers) are journaled with an **effective-tick** at the next day boundary: replays before that tick use old values, after it new ones — tuning becomes part of history instead of corrupting it. This also dissolves the old §14.10 self-contradiction: nothing remote ever alters simulation rules; tuning arrives only inside store-delivered, version-pinned, effective-tick-journaled content.

### 14.7 Content Pipeline & Localization

**Loom Studio is a budgeted product, not an assumption** — 4,900 fragments, the 140-card Mechanism Deck plus Season Kits, and launch-day FIGS+BR-PT all hang off it from month three. Two named tool engineers from month one, staged so the critical path ships first: **M0 (end of month 2)** — content schemas, the headless CLI validator, spreadsheet round-trip authoring; writers are productive before any UI exists. **M1 (month 4)** — coverage heatmaps per (citizen × relationship-class × affect region) cell. **M2 (month 6)** — hot-simulation against recorded runs, one-click precondition fuzzing. **M3 (month 8)** — dual-reading beacon review with two-sign-off tracking: every beacon passes one reader who finds it innocent and one who finds it performed before it locks. The validator, not the UI, is the dependency: orphaned fragments, unreachable affect regions, unsatisfiable preconditions, and cells under the 6-fragment floor are build breaks from M0 onward. Loom Studio embeds the same `LG.Sim` assembly under CoreCLR — exactly why §14.2's golden-replay CI exists.

**Localization is adaptation, not translation — at launch, at an honest price.** Review was right that 320K re-authored words in 14 weeks by two writers was fantasy; the answer is tiering plus headcount, not a quietly slipped promise. The budget is tiered by the coverage heatmap: high-traffic cells — measured from recorded playthroughs, roughly 35% of fragments carrying ~80% of real selection traffic — are **fully re-authored** in-language, affect-tagged, validator-clean; the long tail gets **validated adaptation** (translated, then affect-verified by the same validators). Vocabulary corpora and One-Word replacement sets are always re-authored; the Edit's realism dies in translation. Cost per language: **four writers, 24 weeks** — and **EN, FIGS, and BR-PT ship together at launch**, as §1, §17, §18, and R9 commit: five language pods run in parallel from the M1 heatmaps (480 writer-weeks total), costed against R9's **$450K** line and reconciled at the M6 vendor lock — if the tiered model and the rate card don't meet, R9's budget line moves; the launch promise does not move by accident. FIGS+BR-PT enter LQA at the §17 Content Complete gate. UI chrome uses string tables; the fiction never does.

### 14.9 Night Watch Notifications

Generated entirely on-device, consistent with offline-first and with the fiction that no one else is watching. Speculation runs **in-session** at Sleep Posture commit (§14.2), never as background compute. Scheduling: review rightly killed the exact-alarm plan — `SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM` is policy-restricted to alarm/calendar-class apps, pre-denied on Android 14, and a Play-review rejection waiting to happen. **We design for inexactness**: `AlarmManager.setWindow` with a ±15-minute window — fully fiction-compatible at 1am; an intercepted text was never punctual — with expedited WorkManager fallback. An **OEM power-manager test matrix** (MIUI, ColorOS, EMUI, One UI aggressive modes — the same hardware as MIN tier) carries measured delivery targets: ≥90% within window, and anything that slips past its window is **silently dropped**, never delivered stale — no 9am "1am text." If the player opens the app before the speculated night resolves, scheduled notifications are cancelled and recomputed against the now-true world. `MessagingStyle` rendering; strictly opt-in, per-citizen mutable, and **capped at one notification per night, maximum** — the consent term published to the player in §4, §5.8, §11.7, and §16.1; the scheduler enforces exactly the number the consent screen shows, and tech never exceeds the contract. Silent hours respected unless 1am delivery is explicitly granted. No push infrastructure exists; there is nothing to go down.

### 14.10 Telemetry & Analytics

Two classes, split exactly as R5 locks at M6 — and the split is load-bearing, because the soft-launch go/no-go runs on it. **Operational telemetry** is anonymous, aggregate, and **default-on**, disclosed in plain language at first boot and publicly documented — a surveillance game will not be sneaky about surveillance; the stance is the press story here too. Its schema is narrow: funnel beats, session shape, the Plausibility availability/novelty gates (§14.4), and the symptom-legibility gates — time-to-correct-attribution per symptom class, and post-Receipt change in alibi behavior. **All §17/§18 launch gates run on this class**, so gate statistics are population-sound, never self-selected. **Research telemetry** — richer behavioral traces for design study — is strictly **opt-in** and feeds no gate. Gates feed the tuning tables of §14.6: shipped in content updates, pinned per run, effective-tick journaled. No remote config touches simulation rules — and the architecture now makes that impossible rather than merely forbidden. Batched on-device, flushed on Wi-Fi.

### 14.11 Anti-Spoiler: The Betrayer Cannot Be Datamined — or Farmed

Structural, not cryptographic: there is no betrayer field in shipped data — not encrypted, *absent*. But review caught the second leak: publishing the casting rule as "watch-time leader" puts the formula on Reddit in week one and degrades "whoever you loved most" into "whoever you nominated." So the Understudy **casts from a composite Mirror signature** — watch-time distribution, intervention targeting, prune/keep pattern, Night Watch selections — with thresholds and **hysteresis** (the candidate must lead the composite across a multi-day rolling window, seed-keyed tie-breaks). Weights ship as data; the formula is never documented; and because the composite privileges *revealed* preference over raw gaze, decoy play becomes **content, not exploit**: stare at Casper for three weeks while your edits, prunes, and Night Watch picks orbit Maya, and the Mirror records the split — the ending portrait names it. *You watched one and kept another.* Manipulating the cast is itself a behavior the game answers.

**Eligibility is explicit: eleven citizens are castable — everyone but Edda, who is dead.** Anton and June are *in* the pool, not exceptions to it: their **Performer (Analog)** packages (specced in §5.5) stage the performance through the Blind-Spot itself — Anton performs in witnessed analog behavior, the one performer no device ever recorded; June's sealed ground truth is the performance's deepest cover. If the composite leader is somehow ineligible, the runner-up casts and the displacement is itself Mirror-recorded. The **eleven-betrayer reveal matrix is a budget line**: per citizen, a beacon re-read pass, a latent performance package, and reveal-dialogue assembly, validated every build by a headless reveal-replay harness — costed at seven engineer-weeks plus per-citizen narrative sign-off (the two Performer (Analog) rows reuse the §5.5 spec and the existing belief-overlay machinery rather than device telemetry). Beacons ship in the clear for all twelve, each genuinely dual-readable; reveal dialogue assembles at runtime from Reply Grammar fragments plus the player's own Ledger justifications, so no minable script exists. A dataminer learns what the marketing already says: anyone you love enough can be the one performing for you. AES-256 protects only pre-release Season packs; the twist is computed from the player, and the player isn't in the APK.

### 14.12 The Reach & the Aldermind

Review's hardest fair hit: Act Three's headline pillar was one struct. The architecture:

**CityState runs in fidelity tiers.** Tier 0, whole game: statistical — election polling, market indices, transit load, the news cycle as aggregate curves on a 4-tick cadence, striking the block as weather (a layoff wave is a parameter shift on Theo's debt pressure). Tier 1, on Reach: a **touched edge agentizes** — one precinct's vote-by-mail logistics, one recommendation queue — promoted to entity-level simulation for the act's duration; budget ≤2 concurrent promotions at +15MB and +5µs/tick each, inside the §14.2 envelope.

**A separate Reach Mechanism Deck** (24 mechanisms unlocking in Act Three — inside the 140-card cap, §14.4; same schema, preconditions over CityState) keeps power-through-coincidence intact at city scale: you do not set an election, you reroute one mail truck through a mechanism that is true. **The Aldermind is the city's Ledger reader** — an anomaly model running audits over civic telemetry, the city-scale analogue of the citizens' encounter pass. Reach acts write `CityEvidenceRecord`s, and the escalation ladder is authored and legible: audit flag → sector sweep → Greywater spotlight → referral to Noor's agency, routing city-scale suspicion back into the Confessor. **Memory burn is mechanical, not adjectival**: every Reach act forces prune slots at the next Vigil, charging city power against the game's only currency, exactly as the bible prices it — late, expensive, morally enormous. City Receipts cite aggregate diffs plus sampled human consequences through authored vignettes bound to statistical outcomes: the rounding error rendered as a person, which is the thesis of the whole game.

Therefore the **iOS port is a planned year-two lane in every scenario** — not a contingency, and the one platform plan stated once, matching §1/§17/§18: Android year one, iOS year two — and it is architecture-cheap (<2GB, fully offline). Base recoups across Android + iOS + year-two Seasons by roughly month 30; breakout clears all-in early in year two across platforms and funds the next project's preproduction. The honest read stands and is sharper for it: this is a featuring-dependent, multi-platform business, which is why the premium stance, the exclusivity trade, and the awards positioning are not garnish — they are the distribution strategy.

## 16. Retention Strategy

### 16.1 Hooks by Timescale

**Per session (10–20 min).** Every sitting ends on the Vigil's prune — a Sophie's choice that closes the app on an unresolved feeling — and Sleep Posture, which makes quitting itself a move. The player never leaves at a zero state; the Zeigarnik pull is built into the day structure, not bolted on.

**Overnight — Night Watch.** Opt-in only, off by default. Consent is requested at exactly one trigger: **the first time the player chooses the Sleep Posture "watch over one person"** (§11.7) — the moment the fiction and the feature are the same act. The diegetic scene asks two things: permission, and *when* — "When may I wake you?" The player names their hour. Delivery law: the notification window is **the player's chosen hour intersected with their historically active window; if the intersection is empty, the chosen hour wins** — explicit consent outranks inference, so the feature can never null-intersect itself into silence for morning-commute players. Maximum one notification per night; the content is an intercepted text on the real lock screen. Target opt-in: **≥50% — the launch gate, one figure shared with §17, §18, and R4** — measured at soft launch. The ethics line holds: Night Watch never monetizes, never expires, never punishes being ignored — the same text waits in the Loom tomorrow.

**Per week — the Season turn.** Turns fire on the **7th played day, not the 7th calendar day** — lapsing never costs content, and the eviction wave, the health scare, and the election all wait. Weekly boundaries are where last week's coincidences come home with interest: the natural screenshot-and-tell moments.

**Per lapse — While You Were Dark.** Absence is simulated at low fidelity and returned as a 60–90 second reel (3-day, 7-day, 30-day tiers) with the player's old interventions blooming inside it as a deduction beat; the 30-day reel adds the Murmur wondering whether its guardian was ever real. **Absence converts into content, never into debt.**

**Per run — the Testament and NG+.** The Testament card is the one-tap share artifact, the gaze-cast betrayer keeps every playthrough structurally unspoilable, and the durable discourse loop is *"who was yours?"* NG+ Echoes and the Bequest (§9.4) turn endings into starting conditions.

### 16.2 The Long-Tail Thesis

The day-200 player is held by **systems, not pipeline** — eight ending families, a betrayer cast from each run's gaze, Bequest modifiers, folklore inheritance, the corrupted-Deck repair loop that gives NG+ its own early game (§9.4.2), and Kits that recombine with the simulated cast. Variance has specced depth, not asserted depth: ≥3 beacon sets per citizen, usage-weighted Reply Grammar banks (§9.4.5). And we measure it honestly — the old "% of day-200 actives in NG+" KPI is retired as survivorship-biased. The KPIs: **finisher-to-NG+ conversion within 7 days of credits (target 40%)** and **D30 retention of NG+ starters versus run-1 starters (target: within 10%)**. If NG+ retains like a new game, the thesis is true; if not, no vanity ratio will hide it.

### 16.3 Breathing on a Published Calendar

There is **no per-player adaptive difficulty anywhere in the product.** The game breathes on a fixed, authored cadence identical for every player: Noor's downtown rotations sit on a case calendar authored at season start and **visible in the Ledger from week 4** — players can plan around her absences, which turns the breather into strategy; intake-relief weeks are authored Season-turn beats on the same calendar. Causality Receipts are never mood-selected — they are honest and itemized or they are not Receipts. Because the breathing is universal and fixed, the Mirror's behavioral sample is never contaminated: every portrait is cut from the same weather. Churn-signature telemetry (sessions abandoned mid-Vigil, lapses after interrogations) informs **between-season tuning patches for everyone**, documented in patch notes. We assume every byte of this design will be datamined, and nothing in it embarrasses us when it is — that is the §15.5 brand, enforced here.

Win-back: exactly one channel — the opted-in Night Watch line — and exactly one in-fiction message per lapse. For those who never opt in, **we accept zero direct reach as the cost of the no-manipulation stance**: their pull is organic (Testament cards in friends' feeds, Cast Census press beats, platform re-featuring), and While You Were Dark makes any unprompted return self-rewarding. The revenue model's lapse-return assumptions use organic-only rates — the stance is priced in, not hand-waved.

### 16.4 Community Strategy

Spoiler-proof by design, so strategy leans into discussion. Three loops: the **Testament gallery** (curated dossier cards, the community's comparison engine); the monthly **Cast Census** — aggregate telemetry published as in-fiction municipal statistics, with one hard rule: betrayal data appears **only in gaze-relative aggregate** ("83% of players were betrayed by someone in their top three watched"), never per character — publishing per-character betrayal rates would be an answer key to the Understudy, and official channels hold themselves to the same redaction the clip tool enforces; and **Confessor clips** as the streamer pipeline, with spoiler-safe recording that auto-redacts the betrayer's identity. The Census still publishes the gut-punch on purpose: the most-pruned memory in the world. No leaderboards, ever — ranking players on a game about care is a category error. The community's organizing question stays the Mirror's: not *who played best*, but *who were you*.

### 16.5 Live-Ops Without a Server

The game is **byte-for-byte complete offline**, reconciling the art bible's promise with everything above. Everything diegetic runs on-device: the simulation, Night Watch (local notifications), While You Were Dark, and the breathing calendar — shipped data evaluated against local play history, needing no server because it adapts to no one. Telemetry is opt-in, opportunistic batch upload, and powers exactly two things: between-season tuning and the Cast Census. A player who never connects misses a monthly community artifact and nothing else — and we say so in the listing, because it's on-brand.

## 19. Risks and Mitigations

### R1 — Sim believability fails ("citizens feel like spreadsheets")

**Likelihood: Medium-High. Impact: Fatal.** The architecture exists to retire this: the fail-closed Plausibility Engine, the bounded Reply Grammar. But a fatal risk deserves a gate that can actually fail, so the M10 kill-gate is instrumented, pre-registered at M8 before the slice exists, and run on N≥40 blind testers: (1) unprompted motive attribution in recorded think-alouds, double-coded by independent coders at κ≥0.7, threshold 70% of testers; (2) behavior prediction — testers forecast a named citizen's next-morning action from a forced-choice set and must beat chance by ≥25 points in aggregate; (3) median voluntary non-intervention watch time ≥5 minutes; (4) free recall of ≥4 of 6 citizens by name, not apartment number. Pass requires 3 of 4. A miss triggers a pre-committed three-month systems-only replan before any content scales. We test at M10 because this is the one risk we will not discover late.

### R2 — Twist leaking

**Likelihood: High. Impact: Medium — by design.** The gaze-cast betrayer cannot be spoiled globally; "Sol is the betrayer" is false for most players. Marketing leads with the mechanic itself. Residual datamining risk is absorbed by the week-one/two beacon events authored with two valid readings for all twelve citizens: a known algorithm spoils nothing about *your* run.

### R3 — Premium price resistance on Android

**Likelihood: High. Impact: High.** The free prologue removes purchase risk; the $9.99 anchor is validated in Wave 2 with the plan based at the gate's own 6%. Contingency with its math attached: if Wave 2 lands at 4–6%, we deploy purchasing-power regional pricing ($4.99–6.99 list across ~50 lower-ARPU markets), modeled from the Wave 2 elasticity cell at +40% unit conversion against ~55% of US net — approximately net-neutral revenue with materially larger install and word-of-mouth bases. Majors never fall below $7.99 in year one (a $1.21 net cut requiring +20% conversion to clear, deployed only if the measured elasticity supports it). The model itself is never touched.

### R4 — Notification permission rejection (Night Watch)

**Likelihood: High. Impact: Medium.** Night Watch is strictly additive: the core loop, the Vigil, and *While You Were Dark* fully serve players without it, and *While You Were Dark* specifically recaptures the lapsed players notifications would otherwise reach. The ask is diegetic and earned — framed as a Sleep Posture choice at the first moment a citizen needs overnight watching. Gate ≥50%; below it we move the ask later, not louder. Policy and harm exposure is R8.

### R5 — Ethical/PR exposure of a surveillance fantasy

**Likelihood: Medium. Impact: High.** The critique is the game: watching costs, evidence accrues, the Mirror indicts the player with their own footage, no karma meter launders it. Operationally, the telemetry taxonomy is split publicly and locked at M6: **operational telemetry** (anonymous, aggregate, default-on, documented — crashes, funnel steps, conversion, opt-in rates; nothing fiction-adjacent) versus **opt-in research telemetry** (anything resembling the game's own gaze, such as watch-time distributions). All launch gates run on the former, so the gates are statistically sound *and* the ethics statement is true the day it ships — a hostile journalist gets to pick neither. The Mirror's behavioral signature is computed on-device and never transmitted. The data-ethics statement ships at announce.

### R6 — Mechanism Deck scope creep

**Likelihood: High. Impact: High.** Hard launch cap of 140 with a pre-declared 120-mechanism viable cut line; a single Deck owner with a written per-act budget; every mechanism authored against a fixed template with an isolation test harness so QA cost stays linear; Seasons absorb post-launch appetite at 12–16 per quarter. A content pipeline with a thermostat, not a wishlist.

### R7 — Google featuring does not land

**Likelihood: Medium. Impact: Fatal to the commercial plan.** This is the plan's single biggest dependency, so it is named: $1.2M cannot buy 7M installs at any plausible CPI, and featuring is 3.0M of the install model. Pre-committed responses: (a) the no-featuring P&L is written now — 2.5M year-one installs from pre-reg, creators, and organic → ~150K payers → ~$1.2M net, which does not close on Android alone; therefore (b) the global launch window floats up to two quarters to chase a committed featuring slot, with soft launch sustaining; (c) the M14 meeting asks for co-marketing commitments, not applause; and (d) if no platform commitment exists by M21, we open publisher conversations for UA capital against the audited soft-launch funnel — trading margin for reach is pre-authorized in exactly this scenario and no other.

### R8 — Night Watch policy rejection or real-world incident

**Likelihood: Medium. Impact: High.** Notifications engineered to read as intercepted texts brush against Play's deceptive-behavior and notification policies, and create an obvious incident: a partner reads "I know what you did — Maya" on a shared nightstand at 1am. Mitigations, by specification: game branding (icon plus "LITTLE GODS — Night Watch") is mandatory in every notification title at every immersion level — the fiction lives in the body, the attribution never lies; Android's sensitive-content flag hides body text on locked screens by default; no notification is ever written to read as a credible real-world accusation outside the game frame; an immersion-level setting (Discreet / Standard) is part of the opt-in itself; and the notification channel is silent and lock-screen-visible by default, per the §13.7 spec — Android, not the app, owns channel loudness, so a "real OS sounds" promise at the notification layer is one we cannot technically keep. **Declared here, once, as a deliberate amendment to the bible:** the canonical "real OS notification sounds, then betray them" beat is relocated in-app — on opening the intercepted text, the game replays the user's default notification sound pitched down 30 cents — preserving the betrayal in the only layer we control, and §13.7 is the governing spec. Policy pre-review happens inside the M14 Google engagement, and a PR incident-response plan ships with the launch kit.

### R9 — Localization breaks the One-Word Edit

**Likelihood: Medium. Impact: High if discovered late.** Single-word substitution shatters in inflected languages unless it is an architecture, not a string pass — so it is decided at M6: Edit candidates are stored as pre-inflected surface forms per language, agreement ripples are contained because every reply comes from authored per-language fragments, and the Edit never free-composes text. Scope declared now: EN/FIGS/BR-PT at launch ($450K budgeted, ~250K words including the 4,800-fragment corpus, LQA on the device matrix); JP/KR in live year one; agglutinative languages deferred until the ruleset proves out. Fragment corpora lock per act from M14.

## 20. Why This Game Could Become A Hit

**The demand is proven; the shelf is empty.** Papers, Please sold 5M+ lifetime including a premium mobile port; Disco Elysium cleared 1M in year one; Monument Valley took $14M+ at premium mobile pricing; Reigns moved 2.7M copies on one thumb. Android has produced none of those games natively. LITTLE GODS does not need to create a market — it needs to be the first flagship-quality supply into one that demonstrably pays, on a platform whose owner subsidizes discovery for exactly this kind of title. And where the plan depends on that subsidy, it now says so out loud and carries the fallback in writing.

**The discourse is structural, not lucky.** Word-of-mouth is the only marketing channel premium economics can afford, and it is engineered into the verbs: the One-Word Edit produces confessions ("I changed one word and ended a marriage"), Causality Receipts and the Testament are screenshot-shaped by specification, the Confessor is a streamer setpiece with a fail state worth watching, and the gaze-cast betrayer makes every playthrough structurally unspoilable — "who did YOUR game cast?" cannot be exhausted by coverage, because coverage generates it. The creator persona saves exist so the campaign's central artifact survives contact with how creators actually capture games.

**The economics survive the genre's usual death — and survive arithmetic.** Narrative games die on content cost. Here the epilogues are cut from the player's own kept Vigil recordings — the most emotionally expensive part of the game is nearly free to produce — and post-launch content is priced against published, honest cost caps: 12% Kits and a 30% Arrival, on a block that holds twelve threads forever. The financial spine is reconstructible by any analyst in ten minutes: a published waterfall to $6.05 base net, stated attach to $8.29 blended, a plan based at the same 6% its own gate enforces, and a break-even reached on the multi-year tail every one of its comps actually earned on. The numbers got smaller in this revision; what they bought is that none of them can be knocked over.

**It is shaped like an award and a headline at once.** Systemic character drama is the industry's white whale; the Mirror's behavioral endings and the no-dark-patterns stance give critics and mainstream press two different reasons to write the same name. The scope — twelve lives, one block, one unfinished sentence — is the only one a 19-person team ships at quality in 24 months, which means the bet is concentrated where it belongs: not on whether the audience exists, but on whether the block feels alive. That is the one risk gated — measurably, falsifiably — at month ten, and everything else in this document is built downstream of passing it.
