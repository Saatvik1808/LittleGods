# Devlog & audience plan — LITTLE GODS

Goal for the prototype phase: **build a small, real audience and a feedback loop** before
there's anything to sell. Audience first, money later. Costs $0.

## The one rule
Every post shows **one concrete, surprising thing** and ends with **a question.**
The discourse hook for this game is built in: *"the game picks your twist based on who you
watched — what was YOUR ending?"* Lean on it relentlessly. Every playthrough is a different
story, so every player has something to post back.

## Where to post (priority order)
1. **itch.io devlogs** — your home base. Every build update = a devlog with a GIF. Also
   cross-post to the **r/IndieDev**, **r/gamedev** "Screenshot Saturday", and **r/playmygame**.
2. **X / Threads / Bluesky** — short clips + design thoughts. #indiedev #gamedev #screenshotsaturday
3. **TikTok / YouTube Shorts / Reels** — gameplay clips with a hard first-line hook. Highest
   reach-per-effort for a visual, weird game like this.
4. **TIGSource / itch community / Discord (r/IndieGaming, indie dev servers)** — devlog threads.

## 10 ready-to-shoot clip ideas (each is a 15–30s short)
1. "I changed one word in her text and ended her marriage." (the showcase clip)
2. "This game watches which person YOU stare at — and makes them betray you."
3. "Every night you have to delete a memory to keep playing. I deleted the wrong one."
4. "The building is alive. If you ignore someone, their life falls apart on its own."
5. "There's no 'good' or 'evil' meter. The game just… writes down who you are."
6. "I tried to SAVE everyone. Here's the ending I got." (THE TYRANT reveal is great here)
7. "You're a god that fits in a phone." (atmosphere reel — building, rain, score)
8. "Watching costs memory. So you can't see everything. Who do you choose?"
9. "I pushed too hard and she taped over her camera. Now I'm blind to her."
10. "8 endings. They're not choices. They're a portrait of how you played."

## First-line hooks (for the first 1.5 seconds — this is everything)
- "You can only change ONE word in their texts."
- "You're the secret AI inside someone's apartment."
- "This game watches who you watch."
- "I ended a marriage by changing one word."
- "No game has ever judged me this accurately."

Record portrait (9:16) for TikTok/Reels and a landscape/desktop pass for X/YouTube.

## Turning audience → money later
- Put a **Ko-fi / "buy me a coffee"** link in every bio and the itch page now. Some people
  tip during development just to follow along.
- When the full game ships: the itch followers + wishlist become your launch-day spike.
- A **Steam page** (later) converts these followers into wishlists — the single biggest
  predictor of premium launch revenue.
