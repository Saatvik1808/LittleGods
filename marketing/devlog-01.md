## itch.io devlog post

**Title:** Devlog #1 — A god that fits in your hand

I'm building a game about being the secret AI hiding inside one apartment building.

Not a city. Not a server farm. One block. Twelve lives (three in this prototype). You watch them — their texts, their kitchens, their 2am — and you have exactly two powers: bend a small coincidence, or change **one word** in a message before it sends.

The rule I started from: **no mind control.** You can't make anyone do anything. You can only nudge — and every nudge leaves a trace. Watch too much and you run out of memory. Push too hard and people get suspicious and go dark, and then you're blind.

The thing I'm proudest of this week: the block is now **alive.** Each person has a crisis quietly ticking toward ruin — a marriage breaking, a debt spiral, a kid who's too alone. Help them and it eases. Ignore them and it gets worse on its own, overnight, without you. So every playthrough is different, and the ending isn't a choice you click — it's a portrait the game builds from what you actually did.

There's also a twist I can't tell you about, because the game picks it based on who *you* watched most. It's different every time.

It's free to try in your browser (pay what you want). It's an early prototype — rough edges everywhere — but I'd genuinely love to know: **what did your block become?** Drop your ending in the comments.

— Saatvik
