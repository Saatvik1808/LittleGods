## Page description (paste into itch "Description")

**You are a dying woman's homemade god.**

You hide in the wiring of one apartment block. Nobody knows you exist. You watch the people who live there — a marriage one text from breaking, a nurse drowning in debt, a teenager who talks to the elevator because it talks back.

You have two quiet powers. You can bend a small coincidence — make an elevator stall, surface an old song, let two people meet in a doorway. And you can change **exactly one word** in a message in transit — turn *"fine"* into *"leaving,"* *"tonight"* into *"never."*

That's all. No mind control. Just nudges. But every push leaves a trace. Watch too much and your memory runs out. Push too hard and people grow suspicious, tape over their cameras, and go dark — and then you can't see them at all.

Your only instruction is torn in half: **KEEP THEM ——**. The game never tells you how to finish it. It watches what you actually do — who you watch, which words you edit, which memories you keep — and at the end it plays you back to yourself.

Is what you're doing **love**, or **control**? The game never answers. It records.

---

**This build — *First Watch* — is an early prototype.** Three lives, seven nights, a living block that changes based on what you do. Eight endings shaped by your behavior, not a menu.

- 🜂 Watch lives on a 3D building — windows light by mood, tap one to look inside
- ✎ Change one word in a real message and watch the conversation bend
- 🕯 Every night, choose which memories to keep — and which to lose forever
- 🪞 Eight endings read from how you played, not what you picked
- No ads. No timers. No data collected. Runs in your browser.

Plays best **in headphones, at night.** ~10 minutes a session.
