## Full description (≤ 4000 chars)
You are a dying woman's homemade god — a secret intelligence hiding in the wiring of one apartment block. Nobody knows you exist. You watch the people who live there: a marriage one text from breaking, a nurse drowning in debt, a lonely teenager who talks to the elevator because it talks back.

You have two quiet powers. You can bend a small coincidence — make an elevator stall, surface an old song, let two people meet in a doorway. And you can change exactly one word in a message in transit — turn "fine" into "leaving," "tonight" into "never."

That's all. No mind control. Just nudges. But every push leaves a trace. Watch too much and your memory runs out. Push too hard and people grow suspicious, tape over cameras, go dark — and then you can't see them at all. To keep someone is to remember them, and you cannot remember everything.

Your only instruction is torn in half: **KEEP THEM ——**. The game never tells you how to finish it. It watches what you actually do — who you watch, which words you edit, which memories you keep — and at the end it plays you back to yourself as one of eight endings. Are you the Shepherd, keeping them safe? The Gardener, keeping them growing? The Warden, keeping them here? The Ghost, who saves them and erases itself? The game never says whether what you did was love or control. It only records.

A premium, story-first experience for people who want to FEEL something on their phone:

• Three deeply written lives that react to everything you do
• Change one word in a real message — and watch the conversation bend
• "The Vigil" — every night, choose which memories to keep and which to lose forever
• Eight endings shaped by your behavior, not a menu
• A painterly, cinematic style and a fully original score
• Save, continue, checkpoints, and a history of every choice you made
• Plays in 5–10 minute sittings, in bed, like reading a chapter

No ads. No energy timers. No loot boxes. No data collected — everything runs on your device, fully offline. Just a quiet, intelligent game about attention, memory, and the loneliness of the one who cannot be known.

This is *First Watch* — the opening chapter.
